# Red Lentil Dal with Roasted Cauliflower and Carrots

A deeply spiced, tomato-based red lentil dal — no coconut milk, so the lentils and aromatics carry the whole dish. Served with turmeric-roasted cauliflower and carrots, finished with a cool dollop of Greek yogurt. Makes 2 servings; cook once, eat twice. ~360 calories per serving.

## Ingredients

### Dal (makes 2 servings)

- 0.75 cup red lentils, rinsed
- 1 medium yellow onion, finely diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated (or 1 tsp ground)
- 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes, with juice
- 2 tsp salt-free chicken bouillon powder
- 2 cups water
- 1 tsp avocado oil
- 1 tsp mustard seeds
- 1.5 tsp cumin
- 1.5 tsp ground coriander
- 2 tsp curry powder
- 1 tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp garam masala
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.25-0.5 tsp cayenne (to taste)
- 1 tsp lemon juice, to finish

### Roasted Cauliflower & Carrots (makes 2 servings)

- 1 head cauliflower, cut into florets
- 4 medium carrots, peeled and cut into 0.5-inch rounds
- 1 tsp avocado oil
- 1 tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp coriander
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp curry powder
- 0.25 tsp cayenne

### Topping (per serving)

- 2 tbsp plain Greek yogurt (0%)
- Fresh cilantro, roughly chopped
- 0.5 tsp cumin seeds or garam masala, sprinkled on yogurt
- Extra lemon juice
- Red pepper flakes

**Prep Notes:**

- Red lentils cook fast (15-18 minutes) and break down into a thick porridge-like texture — no soaking needed, no pre-cooking. Rinse thoroughly until water runs clear.
- Mustard seeds need a hot dry pan to pop and release their nutty flavor before adding oil and aromatics. Listen for the popping — that's the signal to proceed.
- Bloom the spices in the oil for 30-60 seconds before adding wet ingredients; this is the single most important step for a dal that tastes like it simmered all day.
- The dal will thicken as it rests. Add a splash of water or broth when reheating day 2.
- Roast the vegetables at high heat (425°F) for caramelized edges — soft, steamed cauliflower doesn't have the same flavor payoff.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Roast Vegetables**:
   - Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment.
   - Toss cauliflower florets and carrot rounds with avocado oil, turmeric, cumin, coriander, garlic powder, cayenne, salt, and pepper.
   - Spread in a single layer. Roast 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway, until edges are browned and caramelized.

2. **Start the Dal**:
   - Heat a medium saucepan over medium-high heat. Add mustard seeds to the dry pan and cook until they start to pop (30-60 seconds).
   - Add avocado oil, then onion. Cook 5-6 minutes until softened and starting to brown.
   - Add garlic and ginger; cook 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
   - Add cumin, coriander, turmeric, smoked paprika, and cayenne. Stir constantly and cook the spices in the aromatics for 60 seconds.

3. **Cook the Lentils**:
   - Add diced tomatoes with their juice. Cook 2-3 minutes, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom.
   - Add rinsed red lentils and vegetable broth. Stir well. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low.
   - Simmer uncovered 15-18 minutes, stirring occasionally, until lentils have broken down and the dal is thick. Add a splash of water if it gets too thick.
   - Stir in garam masala and lemon juice. Taste and adjust salt, lemon, and cayenne.

4. **Serve**:
   - Ladle dal into a bowl. Add a portion of roasted cauliflower and carrots alongside or on top.
   - Dollop Greek yogurt on top. Sprinkle with fresh cilantro, red pepper flakes, and a pinch of cumin or garam masala on the yogurt.

## Notes

- **Calories per serving**: ~360 total (dal ~270 including lentils, tomatoes, aromatics + roasted vegetables ~60 + Greek yogurt ~25).
- **Two Meals**: Store the second serving of dal and vegetables separately in the fridge. Reheat in a saucepan with a splash of water; yogurt goes on fresh each time.
