# Chicken Noodle Soup

Classic shredded chicken noodle soup rebuilt for low-calorie eating — miracle noodles replace pasta, salt-free bouillon builds rich flavor without sodium overload, and extra vegetables stretch the bowl without stretching the calories. Warming, high-volume, deeply satisfying. ~280 calories.

## Ingredients

### Broth Base

- 1 boneless, skinless chicken breast (6 oz)
- 3 cups water
- 2 tsp salt-free chicken bouillon powder
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp onion powder
- 0.5 tsp turmeric (adds color and anti-inflammatory benefit, subtle flavor)
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 1 bay leaf

### Vegetables

- 3 stalks celery, sliced into 0.25-inch pieces
- 2 medium carrots, peeled and sliced into rounds
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp avocado oil (for sautéing aromatics)

### Noodles

- 1 package (7-8 oz) miracle noodles (shirataki noodles), rinsed and drained well, ~10-15 cal

### Finishing

- 1 tbsp dill
- Extra black pepper
- Red pepper flakes (optional)
- Squeeze of lemon juice (brightens the broth)

**Prep Notes:**

- Miracle noodles require a thorough rinse under cold water for at least 30 seconds — they come packed in liquid with a strong odor that disappears completely once rinsed.
- For best texture, dry-toast the miracle noodles in a dry skillet for 2-3 minutes after rinsing; this removes remaining moisture and gives them a slightly chewier bite that mimics pasta better.
- Poach the chicken directly in the seasoned broth — this both cooks the chicken and infuses the broth with chicken flavor simultaneously.
- Shred the chicken very finely for classic chicken noodle texture; two forks work well, or cool slightly and pull apart with hands.
- Salt-free bouillon varies widely in potency by brand — start with less and taste before adding more.
- Add miracle noodles to the finished soup at the very end — they are already cooked and only need to warm through.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Sauté Aromatics**:
   - Heat avocado oil in a medium saucepan or Dutch oven over medium heat.
   - Add onion and cook 4-5 minutes until translucent. Add garlic and cook 1 minute.

2. **Build the Broth**:
   - Add carrots, celery, garlic powder, onion powder, thyme, parsley, turmeric, salt, pepper, and bay leaf. Stir to coat vegetables in the aromatics.
   - Add water and bouillon powder. Stir well to dissolve.

3. **Poach Chicken**:
   - Nestle the whole chicken breast into the broth (it will be partially submerged; that is fine).
   - Bring to a gentle simmer over medium heat. Cook 15-18 minutes until chicken is cooked through (165°F internal). Remove chicken and let rest 5 minutes.
   - Shred with two forks into thin strips. Return to the pot.

4. **Prep Miracle Noodles**:
   - While chicken poaches, rinse miracle noodles thoroughly under cold water for 30 seconds.
   - In a dry skillet over medium heat, add noodles and cook 2-3 minutes, stirring, until mostly dry and slightly chewy. Set aside.

5. **Finish the Soup**:
   - Remove bay leaf. Squeeze in lemon juice. Taste and adjust bouillon, salt, and pepper.
   - Add miracle noodles to the soup and stir to combine. Let warm for 1-2 minutes.

6. **Serve**:
   - Ladle into a large bowl. Top with fresh parsley or dill, extra black pepper, and red pepper flakes.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~280 total (chicken ~160 + vegetables ~60 + miracle noodles ~15 + aromatics/oil ~45).
- **Miracle Noodles**: Shirataki noodles are made from konjac glucomannan and are essentially calorie-free. The dry-toasting step is non-optional for good texture — skipping it leaves rubbery, wet noodles.
- **Volume**: This soup is high volume relative to calories — a large bowl is deeply filling thanks to the broth, vegetables, and noodles. The broth alone creates a satiety signal that solid food of the same caloric value would not.
- **Salt-Free Bouillon**: Standard bouillon is very high sodium. Salt-free versions (like Herb-Ox or Frontier Co-op) let you control sodium while still getting a concentrated savory chicken flavor. Use generously.
- **Turmeric**: Adds golden color (more visually appetizing than pale broth), mild earthy flavor, and anti-inflammatory curcumin. Not detectable as "turmeric" in the final soup — just golden color and depth.
