# Thai Green Curry Pork Stir Fry

Thinly sliced pork loin and a heap of vegetables — cabbage, snap peas, mushrooms, bell pepper, carrots — tossed in a bold green curry coconut sauce with miracle noodles soaking up every drop. Intentionally very saucy. Makes 3 servings — cook once, eat three times. ~370 calories per serving.

## Ingredients

### Pork

- 12 oz pork loin, thinly sliced against the grain into bite-size strips
- 0.5 tbsp avocado oil
- 1 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce (for velveting)
- 1 tsp cornstarch (for velveting)

### Vegetables

- 1 head green cabbage, thinly shredded
- 8 oz mushrooms, sliced
- 1 large red bell pepper, thinly sliced
- 1.5 cups snap peas, trimmed
- 2 medium carrots, sliced into thin coins or julienned
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 inch fresh ginger, grated or minced
- 3 green onions, sliced (whites and greens separated)

### Noodles

- 1 package (14-16 oz) miracle noodles (shirataki), drained and rinsed well

### Sauce

- 2.5 tbsp green thai curry paste (adjust to heat preference)
- 1 can (13.5 oz) lite coconut milk
- 2 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
- 1 tbsp fish sauce
- 1 tbsp lime juice, plus more to finish
- 1 tsp honey or brown sugar
- 0.5 tsp sesame oil (finishing)
- 0.25 cup low-sodium chicken broth (to thin sauce if needed)

### Garnish

- Lime wedges
- Extra green onion tops
- Fresh cilantro or Thai basil
- Red pepper flakes or chili oil

**Prep Notes:**

- Velvet the pork: toss the strips with soy sauce and cornstarch and let sit 10-15 minutes before cooking. This coats the meat in a thin protective layer that keeps it tender under high heat and helps the sauce cling.
- Rinse miracle noodles very thoroughly under cold water — the packaging liquid has a strong odor that disappears with proper rinsing. After rinsing, pat dry and dry-toast them in the pan for 2-3 minutes before combining with the rest of the dish. This step significantly improves texture.
- Mix all sauce ingredients in a bowl before starting so you can add it in one smooth move at the right moment. The sauce will look thin but thickens quickly once it hits the hot pan.
- Keep the cabbage shredded thin — it wilts fast and adds bulk without calories.
- This makes 3 servings — portion evenly into storage containers after cooking.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Marinate Pork**:
   - Toss pork strips with 1 tbsp soy sauce and 1 tsp cornstarch. Set aside at least 10 minutes.

2. **Prep Miracle Noodles**:
   - Drain and rinse under cold water for 30 seconds. Pat dry with a towel.
   - Heat a dry wok or skillet over medium-high. Add noodles and toast 2-3 minutes, stirring, until slightly dried out and squeaky. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.

3. **Sear Pork**:
   - Heat avocado oil in the wok over high heat until nearly smoking.
   - Add pork in a single layer. Cook undisturbed 2 minutes until browned. Stir and cook 1-2 more minutes until just cooked through.
   - Transfer to a plate.

4. **Stir Fry Vegetables**:
   - In the same wok over high heat, add carrots and mushrooms. Stir fry 3 minutes until starting to soften and take on color.
   - Add garlic, ginger, and green onion whites to the center of the pan. Cook 30-60 seconds until fragrant, then stir into the vegetables.
   - Add bell pepper and snap peas. Stir fry 2 minutes — keep heat high, keep things moving.
   - Add cabbage. Toss to incorporate and cook 1-2 minutes until just beginning to wilt.

5. **Build the Sauce**:
   - Add the curry paste directly to the vegetables. Stir and cook 30-60 seconds to bloom the paste in the residual oil.
   - Pour in the full sauce (coconut milk, soy sauce, fish sauce, lime juice, honey). Stir well to combine everything.
   - Bring to a simmer and cook 2-3 minutes until slightly reduced.
   - Add chicken broth if the sauce is too thick.

6. **Combine**:
   - Return seared pork and the toasted miracle noodles to the wok. Toss everything together until well-coated.
   - Cook 1 more minute so the noodles absorb sauce.
   - Remove from heat. Drizzle with sesame oil and a squeeze of extra lime.

7. **Serve**:
   - Divide into 3 portions. Top with green onion tops, cilantro or Thai basil, and chili oil or red pepper flakes.
   - **Reheating**: The sauce thickens significantly overnight. Add a splash of water or broth and reheat over medium, stirring to loosen.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~370 per serving (pork ~200 + lite coconut milk ~65 + vegetables ~80 + sauce and oil ~25) — miracle noodles add no meaningful calories while soaking up sauce.
- Green curry paste varies significantly in heat level by brand. Mae Ploy and Maesri run hotter; Mae Anong and Aroy-D run milder. Start at 2 tbsp and taste-test before adding the full amount if unsure.
- The sauce is intentionally abundant — the cabbage and noodles drink it up.
