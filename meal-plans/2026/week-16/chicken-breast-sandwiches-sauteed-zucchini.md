# Chicken Breast Sandwiches with Sautéed Zucchini

One pan-seared herb-rubbed chicken breast butterflied into two halves, each on a toasted bun with melted provolone and fresh tomato, alongside garlicky sautéed zucchini. Two servings from one breast. ~490 calories per serving (sandwich + side).

## Ingredients

### Chicken

- 1 large boneless skinless chicken breast (about 8 oz), butterflied into 2 even halves
- 0.5 tbsp avocado oil
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.75 tsp garlic powder
- 0.5 tsp Italian seasoning
- 0.5 tsp onion powder
- 0.25 tsp black pepper
- 0.25 tsp salt

### Sandwiches

- 2 sturdy sandwich buns or ciabatta rolls, toasted
- 2 slices provolone or Swiss cheese
- 4 thick slices ripe tomato
- 2 leaves romaine or green leaf lettuce

### Spread

- 4-5 oil-packed sun-dried tomatoes, finely minced
- 3 tbsp plain low-fat Greek yogurt
- 1 clove garlic, minced or grated
- 0.5 tsp olive oil (from the sun-dried tomato jar)
- 0.25 tsp smoked paprika
- Pinch of red pepper flakes
- Salt and black pepper to taste

### Sautéed Zucchini Side

- 2 medium zucchini, sliced into 0.25-inch rounds
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 0.5 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.25 tsp red pepper flakes
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- 1 tbsp lemon juice (finishing)
- 2 tbsp fresh parsley or basil, roughly chopped (finishing)

**Prep Notes:**

- To butterfly: lay the breast flat and slice horizontally through the middle almost all the way through, then open it like a book. Cut fully through to make two separate halves. Each half is a sandwich portion. Pound each half lightly to even thickness before seasoning.
- Pat chicken completely dry before applying the spice rub; surface moisture prevents browning.
- Mix the spread ahead of time: mince the sun-dried tomatoes as fine as possible, then stir together with yogurt, garlic, olive oil from the jar, smoked paprika, and red pepper flakes. Refrigerate until ready to assemble — the flavors sharpen as it sits.
- Slice zucchini into consistent rounds so they cook at the same rate. Don't crowd the pan — cook in two batches if needed. Crowding steams instead of sears.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Season Chicken**:
   - Combine smoked paprika, garlic powder, Italian seasoning, onion powder, salt, and black pepper in a small bowl.
   - Pat both halves dry. Rub the spice mix evenly over both sides of each half.

2. **Sear Chicken**:
   - Heat avocado oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering.
   - Add both halves. Cook undisturbed 3-4 minutes until deeply golden brown.
   - Flip, reduce heat to medium. Cook 3-4 more minutes until internal temperature reaches 165°F. The halves are thinner than a whole breast — don't overcook.
   - In the last minute, lay a slice of provolone on each half and cover the pan loosely to melt.
   - Transfer to a cutting board and rest 3 minutes.

3. **Sauté Zucchini** (start while chicken rests):
   - In the same skillet (wipe clean) or a second pan, heat olive oil over medium-high.
   - Add zucchini in a single layer. Cook undisturbed 2-3 minutes until golden on the bottom.
   - Stir, push to the edges. Add garlic to the center and cook 30-60 seconds until fragrant.
   - Toss everything together. Season with smoked paprika, red pepper flakes, salt, and pepper.
   - Cook 2 more minutes until zucchini is tender-crisp and well-colored.
   - Remove from heat. Add lemon juice and fresh herbs, toss to coat.

4. **Assemble Sandwiches**:
   - Toast buns until golden.
   - Spread the sun-dried tomato spread on the bottom bun.
   - Layer: lettuce, chicken half with melted cheese, tomato slices.
   - Serve with sautéed zucchini on the side.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~405 per sandwich + ~85 for zucchini side = ~490 total per serving (chicken ~170 + bun ~150 + cheese ~80 + tomato ~20 + oil and spices ~70 + zucchini ~85) — varies slightly with spread choice.
- Both halves cook at the same time and finish quickly — the butterflied halves cook in roughly half the time of a full breast.
