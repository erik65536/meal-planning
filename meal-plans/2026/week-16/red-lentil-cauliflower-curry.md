# Red Lentil Cauliflower Curry

A thick, warming curry built on creamy red lentils and tender cauliflower with a deeply spiced base of fenugreek, garam masala, curry powder, and fresh aromatics. Spinach and fire-roasted tomatoes round it out. Makes 3 generous servings — cook once, eat three times. ~385 calories per serving.

## Ingredients

### Aromatics

- 0.5 tbsp avocado oil
- 1 large red bell pepper, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1.5 inches fresh ginger, minced or grated

### Lentils & Vegetables

- 1 cup dry red lentils, rinsed until water runs clear
- 1 small head cauliflower, cut into small florets (about 4 cups)
- 2 medium carrots, diced into 0.5-inch pieces
- 1 medium sweet potato, diced into 0.5-inch pieces (skin on)
- 3 cups baby spinach, loosely packed
- 1 cup frozen peas
- 1 can (28 oz) fire-roasted diced tomatoes
- 2 cups low-sodium vegetable broth
- 0.5 cup water (add more as needed to control thickness)

### Spices

- 1.5 tsp curry powder
- 1.5 tsp garam masala
- 1 tsp ground fenugreek
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 0.75 tsp turmeric
- 0.5 tsp ground coriander
- 0.5 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.25 tsp cayenne (or more to taste)
- 0.5 tsp salt (adjust to taste)

### Finishing

- 1.5 tbsp lemon juice
- 0.5 cup lite coconut milk or 0.25 cup plain low-fat Greek yogurt (for creaminess)
- Fresh cilantro, roughly chopped

**Prep Notes:**

- Red lentils cook to a creamy, thick consistency in about 20 minutes without soaking. They naturally dissolve into the curry as they cook, thickening the whole pot. Pull off heat when they've softened but still hold loose shape — fully dissolved is fine for texture but watch the consistency.
- Cut cauliflower into small, uniform florets (about 1 inch) so they cook through at the same rate as the lentils.
- Blooming the spices in oil with the aromatics (step 2) is the most important step — it dramatically deepens flavor compared to adding spices with the liquid. Ground fenugreek is slightly bitter on its own but rounds out beautifully when bloomed in fat. Don't rush this step.
- The curry thickens significantly in the fridge. Add a generous splash of broth when reheating nights 2 and 3.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Sauté Aromatics**:
   - Heat avocado oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat.
   - Add bell pepper and carrots. Cook 4-5 minutes until slightly softened.
   - Add garlic and ginger. Cook 1 minute, stirring, until fragrant.

2. **Bloom Spices**:
   - Add curry powder, garam masala, fenugreek, cumin, turmeric, coriander, smoked paprika, and cayenne directly into the aromatics.
   - Stir constantly for 60-90 seconds over medium heat until the spices are fragrant and coat the vegetables. The pan will look dry — that's fine. Don't let the spices burn.

3. **Add Tomatoes & Lentils**:
   - Pour in the canned tomatoes. Stir well to deglaze the pan, scraping up any spices stuck to the bottom.
   - Add rinsed red lentils, sweet potato, vegetable broth, and water. Stir to combine.
   - Bring to a boil.

4. **Add Cauliflower & Simmer**:
   - Once boiling, add cauliflower florets and stir to submerge.
   - Reduce heat to medium-low. Cover and simmer 20-25 minutes, stirring every 5 minutes, until lentils have broken down into a thick base and cauliflower and sweet potato are fork-tender.
   - If it's getting too thick before the vegetables are cooked, add water 0.25 cup at a time.

5. **Finish**:
   - Add spinach and frozen peas. Stir in and cook 2 minutes until spinach is wilted and peas are heated through.
   - Stir in lite coconut milk or Greek yogurt.
   - Add lemon juice. Taste and adjust salt, cayenne, and garam masala.
   - If too thick, add a splash of broth and stir. If too thin, simmer uncovered 5 minutes.

6. **Serve**:
   - Ladle into bowls. Top with fresh cilantro and an extra squeeze of lemon.
   - **Reheating**: Add 0.25-0.5 cup broth or water per portion and stir over medium-low until loosened and heated through.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~385 per serving (lentils ~230 + cauliflower ~55 + vegetables ~55 + tomatoes ~30 + oil ~30 + coconut milk ~35) — varies slightly based on coconut milk vs. yogurt finish.
- Ground fenugreek (methi) contributes a distinctive, slightly bitter depth with faint maple undertones. It's a foundational flavor in many Indian curry bases — don't substitute or skip it.
- This curry is fully vegan as written with coconut milk; the Greek yogurt option adds protein and a tangier finish.
- Flavor peaks on day 2 — the spices continue to develop overnight.
