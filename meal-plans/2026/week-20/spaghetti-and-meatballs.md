# Spaghetti and Meatballs

Tender, boldly seasoned beef meatballs built on a panade — soft and juicy, not dense — in a chunky Italian-American tomato sauce over shirataki spaghetti. Double the classic herbs and spices: assertive fennel, oregano, and garlic throughout, simmered until the sauce goes jammy and coating. ~1,050 calories.

## Ingredients

### Meatballs

- 8 oz ground beef, 80/20
- 2 slice white or whole wheat bread, torn into small pieces
- 3 tbsp whole milk
- 1 egg
- 4 cloves garlic, finely grated on a microplane
- 0.25 cup fresh parsley, finely chopped
- 2 tsp dried oregano
- 1 tsp fennel seeds, crushed in a mortar or under a heavy pan
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp onion powder
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 0.5 tsp red pepper flakes
- 0.75 tsp black pepper
- 0.5 tsp salt

### Tomato Sauce

- 1 can (14.5 oz) whole peeled tomatoes, hand-crushed
- 1 medium fresh tomato, finely diced
- 0.5 small onion, finely diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tbsp tomato paste
- 2 tsp dried oregano
- 1 tsp dried basil
- 1 tsp fennel seeds, crushed
- 0.5 tsp red pepper flakes
- 0.5 tsp black pepper
- Salt to taste
- 6-8 fresh basil leaves, torn (added at end)

### Noodles

- 1 package (7-8 oz) shirataki spaghetti noodles, rinsed and drained

### Finishing

- Fresh parsley, chopped
- Fresh basil, torn
- [Optional] Grated Parmesan or Pecorino Romano
- Red pepper flakes to taste

**Prep Notes:**

- **Panade**: Combine torn bread and milk in a large bowl and mash with a fork into a smooth, uniform paste. Let sit 3 minutes before mixing into the beef. This is what keeps meatballs from turning dense.
- **Mixing**: Fold the meatball mixture together until just combined — stop the moment everything is incorporated. Overworking activates proteins and toughens the meatballs.
- **Garlic in meatballs**: Grate on a microplane rather than mincing so it disappears evenly into the meat without visible chunks.
- **Hand-crushing tomatoes**: Hold each whole canned tomato over the pot and squeeze to crush by hand, leaving uneven pieces for texture. Pour all the juice from the can in too.
- **Shirataki noodles**: Non-negotiable step — drain, rinse under cold water 30 seconds, then dry-fry in a hot dry skillet over medium-high for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly, until squeaky and mostly dry. Skipping this leaves rubbery, odorous noodles.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Make the panade.** In a large bowl, combine torn bread pieces and milk. Mash with a fork into a smooth paste. Let sit 3 minutes.

2. **Mix and form meatballs.** Add ground beef, egg, grated garlic, parsley, oregano, fennel seeds, smoked paprika, onion powder, garlic powder, red pepper flakes, black pepper, and salt to the panade. Mix gently by hand until just combined. With damp hands, roll into 1.5-inch balls (yields 12-14 meatballs). Set aside.

3. **Brown meatballs.** Heat 1 tsp avocado oil in a large deep skillet over medium-high heat. Add meatballs with space between them. Brown on all sides, turning every 60-90 seconds, about 3-4 minutes total. They don't need to be cooked through. Remove and set aside on a plate.

4. **Build the sauce.** Reduce heat to medium. Add 1 tbsp olive oil to the same pan. Add onion and cook 5-7 minutes until soft and golden. Add garlic and tomato paste and stir constantly for 1-2 minutes until the paste darkens slightly and smells nutty. Add hand-crushed canned tomatoes with all their juices and the fresh diced tomato. Stir in oregano, basil, fennel seeds, red pepper flakes, and black pepper.

5. **Simmer.** Nestle the browned meatballs back into the sauce. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce to a low simmer. Cook uncovered 35-40 minutes, stirring gently every 10 minutes, until the sauce thickens and the meatballs are fully cooked through. Taste and adjust salt.

6. **Prep the noodles.** While the sauce simmers, drain and rinse shirataki noodles under cold water for 30 seconds. Dry-fry in a hot skillet over medium-high heat (no oil) for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly, until squeaky and dry. Season lightly with salt.

7. **Finish.** Stir torn fresh basil into the sauce. Add more red pepper flakes or salt to taste.

8. **Serve.** Plate the shirataki noodles and spoon sauce and meatballs generously over top. Garnish with fresh parsley, fresh basil, Parmesan if using, and a pinch of red pepper flakes.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~1,050 total (beef 80/20 ~570, egg ~70, bread ~80, milk ~28, shirataki noodles ~15, olive oil ~120, avocado oil ~40, canned tomatoes ~60, fresh tomato ~20, onion, garlic, tomato paste, seasonings ~50). Skip Parmesan to hold the line; add it for ~55 more.
- **80/20 beef**: The fat content keeps the meatballs juicy and renders into the sauce during simmering, adding body. Blot the meatballs briefly after browning before returning to sauce if you want to cut back.
- **35-40 minute simmer**: This is the threshold where the sauce transitions from bright and acidic to something deeper and sweeter. Don't rush it.
- **Whole peeled tomatoes vs. crushed**: Hand-crushing whole tomatoes gives irregular texture — some broken down into sauce, some still in small chunks. Much better than pre-crushed for this style of sauce.
