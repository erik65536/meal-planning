# Bistro Burger Board — Pâté & Egg-Latke

Two gourmet lettuce-wrapped burgers from the same 8 oz of 80/20 ground beef, served side by side on a board. **Burger 1**: a French bistro pâté burger — warm patty spread with chicken liver pâté and melted Gruyère, caramelized onions, sliced tomato, thin Granny Smith apple slices, and Dijon, wrapped in butter lettuce. **Burger 2**: an egg-latke burger — a potato latke as the base, patty layered with Gruyère, crowned with a runny sunny-side egg, pickled jalapeños, and a Greek yogurt-chive dollop; the yolk breaks over everything and becomes the sauce. Served with air-fried fingerling potatoes and tricolor bell peppers. ~1,500 calories total (indulgent Sunday meal).

## Ingredients

### Burger Patties (both)

- 8 oz ground beef, 80/20, divided into two 4 oz portions
- 0.75 tsp kosher salt
- 0.5 tsp coarse black pepper
- 0.25 tsp garlic powder
- 0.25 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce, pressed into both patties before cooking

### Gruyère (both burgers)

- 2 slices Gruyère, 0.5 oz each

### Burger 1 — Pâté

- 2 tbsp chicken liver pâté (or duck pâté), store-bought, at room temperature
- 1 tbsp Dijon mustard (or 0.5 tbsp Dijon + 0.5 tbsp whole grain mustard)
- 0.5 cup caramelized onions (see Prep Notes)
- 3-4 thin slices fresh tomato
- 4-5 thin slices Granny Smith apple (peeled, cut just before assembly to prevent browning)
- 2 large butter lettuce leaves, outer, cupped

### Burger 2 — Egg-Latke

- 1 medium russet potato (~6 oz), peeled and grated
- 0.25 small yellow onion, grated
- 1 egg (for latke batter)
- 1 tbsp flour or matzo meal
- Salt and black pepper
- Avocado oil spray (or 0.5 tsp avocado oil) for the latke pan
- 1 egg, fried sunny-side up (for topping)
- Pickled jalapeño slices (jarred), 6-8 slices
- 1 tbsp Greek yogurt + 1 tsp fresh chives, chopped (dollop)
- 1 large butter lettuce leaf (draped over top as a handle)

### Air-Fried Potatoes and Bell Peppers

- 6 oz fingerling potatoes, halved lengthwise
- 1 small red bell pepper, cut into thick strips
- 1 small orange or yellow bell pepper, cut into thick strips
- 1 tbsp avocado oil
- 0.5 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.25 tsp garlic powder
- 0.25 tsp onion powder
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- [Optional] 1 sprig fresh rosemary, stripped
- Balsamic glaze, drizzle to finish

**Prep Notes:**

- **Caramelized onions**: Thinly slice 1 medium yellow onion. Heat 1 tsp olive oil in a small skillet over the lowest heat possible. Add onion and a pinch of salt. Cook undisturbed 5 minutes, stir, then continue cooking 35-45 minutes total, stirring every 8-10 minutes, until deep amber and jammy. Do not rush with higher heat — they will burn before caramelizing. Can be made the day before and reheated.
- **Apple slices**: Cut Granny Smith apple thin (about 0.1 inch) just before assembly. The tartness contrasts the richness of the pâté and Gruyère — a classic French charcuterie pairing.
- **Latke moisture**: After grating the potato and onion, wrap tightly in a clean kitchen towel and wring hard — as much moisture as possible must go. Press onto paper towels and wring again after 2 minutes. Even with minimal oil, removing moisture is the most important factor for texture.
- **Latke size**: Press the latke to match the burger patty's diameter (~3.5 inches) so it serves as a proper base. Aim for 0.5-inch thickness before it cooks down. Thinner latkes crack under the weight of the burger.
- **Patty dimple**: Press a shallow thumb dimple in the center of each raw patty — this prevents the burger from doming as it cooks and keeps toppings from sliding off.
- **Pâté temperature**: Remove pâté from the fridge 20-30 minutes before cooking. Cold pâté tears when spread and tastes muted. Room-temperature pâté spreads like butter and has full flavor.
- **Butter lettuce**: Use only the large outer leaves. They cup naturally and wrap around the burger without tearing. Have 1-2 extras per burger on hand.

## Cooking Order

| Time | Task |
|------|------|
| T-45 min | Start caramelized onions |
| T-25 min | Start air fryer potatoes and peppers |
| T-15 min | Make latke batter; fry latke |
| T-8 min | Cook burger patties; melt Gruyère |
| T-2 min | Fry egg |
| T-0 | Assemble and plate |

## Cooking Instructions

### Caramelized Onions
1. Slice onion thin. Heat 1 tsp olive oil in a small skillet over lowest heat. Add onion and a pinch of salt. Cook 40-45 minutes, stirring every 8-10 minutes, until deep amber. Season and set aside.

### Air-Fried Potatoes and Bell Peppers
1. Toss fingerling potatoes and bell pepper strips with avocado oil, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, pepper, and rosemary if using.
2. Air fry at 400°F for 22-25 minutes, shaking the basket halfway, until potatoes are golden and crispy at the edges and peppers have charred tips. Finish with a thin drizzle of balsamic glaze immediately out of the fryer.

### Latke
1. Combine wrung-dry grated potato and onion in a bowl. Add egg, flour, a good pinch of salt, and black pepper. Mix well.
2. Spray or brush a light coat of avocado oil (0.5 tsp or a 2-second spray) in a medium nonstick skillet over medium-high heat. Scoop all the batter into the pan and press into a round, ~3.5 inches across. Cook undisturbed 4-5 minutes until the underside is set and golden. Flip carefully and cook another 4-5 minutes. The exterior will be lightly golden rather than deeply crispy — that's expected with minimal oil. Remove to a wire rack.
3. Keep the skillet for the burgers.

### Burger Patties
1. Season both patties all over with salt, pepper, garlic powder, and smoked paprika. Press a thumb dimple in the center of each. Press 0.5 tsp Worcestershire into the top of each.
2. Heat the skillet (latke residue left in) over medium-high. Add a small drizzle of avocado oil if the pan looks dry. Sear patties 3-4 minutes per side for medium (135-140°F internal). Do not press down on them.
3. In the final 60 seconds, lay a slice of Gruyère on each patty. Cover the pan with a lid for 45 seconds to trap steam and melt the cheese completely. Rest 3 minutes off heat.

### Fried Egg
1. In a small nonstick pan over medium-low heat, heat a small drizzle of avocado oil. Crack egg gently into pan. Season with salt and black pepper. Cook 3 minutes until white is fully set but yolk is still completely runny — do not flip. The yolk should jiggle freely when you shake the pan.

### Assembly

**Burger 1 — Pâté:**
- Lay two cupped butter lettuce leaves on the board, overlapping slightly, open side facing up.
- Smear Dijon across the inside of the lettuce.
- Place the rested patty (Gruyère side up) in the center.
- Spread pâté generously across the warm Gruyère with a small spoon or offset spatula — the warmth softens it slightly.
- Spoon caramelized onions over the pâté.
- Lay tomato slices over the onions, then fan the apple slices over the tomato.
- Fold the lettuce leaves up and around the sides, pressing gently to hold.

**Burger 2 — Egg-Latke:**
- Set the latke flat on the board as the base.
- Stack the rested patty (Gruyère side up) centered on the latke.
- Slide the fried egg directly on top of the Gruyère, yolk centered.
- Arrange pickled jalapeño slices around the yolk like a halo.
- Dollop Greek yogurt with chives at the edge of the egg, or off to the side of the plate.
- Drape one large butter lettuce leaf over the top — it won't fully enclose and isn't meant to; it serves as a handle to press down and eat.

### Board Presentation
- Position both burgers side-by-side on a large wooden board or wide plate — pâté burger on the left, egg-latke on the right.
- Arrange fingerling potatoes and bell pepper strips across the bottom of the board, fanning the tricolor pepper strips for maximum visual contrast.
- Scatter a few reserved apple slices and tomato slices between the burgers as garnish.
- Place the small bowl of Greek yogurt-chive dip (if any remains) at the corner of the board.
- Finish with a pinch of flaky salt over both burgers and the potatoes.
- When eating burger 2, press down on the lettuce leaf — the yolk breaks and pools down through the Gruyère and into the latke. That is the moment.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~1,500 total — Burger 1 ~530 (patty ~295, Gruyère ~55, pâté ~80, caramelized onion ~65, tomato ~10, apple ~20, mustard/lettuce ~5); Burger 2 ~670 (patty ~295, Gruyère ~55, latke ~230 with minimal oil, egg ~90, jalapeño/yogurt/lettuce ~15); Side ~310 (fingerlings ~150, peppers ~60, avocado oil ~100).
- **The yolk is the sauce**: Do not add other sauces to burger 2 beyond the yogurt dollop. When the yolk breaks it coats everything — adding more muddies it.
- **Pâté options**: Chicken liver pâté is the most accessible (most specialty grocery stores carry it). Duck pâté is richer and more complex. Either works. Braunschweiger is a widely available alternative if pâté is hard to find.
- **Latke stability**: The latke will soften slightly under the warm burger. If you want it to hold its crunch longer, let it cool 5 minutes on the rack before stacking the patty — the crust sets as it cools.
- **Caramelized onion yield**: One medium onion yields roughly 0.5 cup caramelized — exactly what burger 1 needs. It shrinks dramatically; this is normal. The flavor is deeply concentrated.
- **The apple**: Granny Smith is essential — a sweet apple (Honeycrisp, Fuji) will just taste like dessert against the pâté. The tart-savory contrast is what makes it work.
- **Color on the board**: Golden caramelized onions, red tomato, pale green apple fan on burger 1. White egg white framing an orange yolk, golden latke, green jalapeño halo on burger 2. Orange and red bell pepper strips across the bottom. It photographs well and eats better.
