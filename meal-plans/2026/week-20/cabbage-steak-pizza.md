# Cabbage Steak Pizza

Two thick cabbage steaks seared until caramelized, then sauced and steamed until just tender, then loaded with bold Italian-American toppings and broiled until the mozzarella is bubbly and spotty brown. Very saucy — sauce goes in twice. The cabbage functions like a thick, hearty crust that absorbs the tomato and holds everything together. ~620 calories.

## Ingredients

### Cabbage Steaks

- 2 thick slices green cabbage, ~1.25 inches each, cut straight through the core (core stays attached and holds the leaves together)
- 1 tsp avocado oil
- Salt, black pepper, and garlic powder for seasoning

### Quick Pizza Sauce

- 1 can (14.5 oz) whole peeled tomatoes, hand-crushed (or 0.75 cup good jarred pizza/marinara sauce)
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 0.5 tsp dried oregano
- 0.5 tsp dried basil
- 0.25 tsp fennel seeds, crushed
- 0.25 tsp red pepper flakes
- 0.25 tsp black pepper
- 0.5 tsp olive oil
- Pinch of salt

### Chicken

- 1 boneless skinless chicken breast (~6 oz), thinly sliced into 0.25-inch strips
- 0.5 tsp Italian seasoning
- 0.25 tsp garlic powder
- 0.25 tsp smoked paprika
- Salt and black pepper
- 0.5 tsp avocado oil

### Toppings

- 1.5 oz low-moisture part-skim mozzarella, shredded or torn into pieces
- 0.5 cup cremini or white mushrooms, thinly sliced
- 0.5 medium red or orange bell pepper, roasted and sliced into strips (see Prep Notes)
- 1 tbsp sun-dried tomatoes (oil-packed), roughly chopped
- 6-8 banana pepper rings (jarred), drained
- [Optional] 6-8 black olives, halved
- Red pepper flakes to taste
- 6-8 fresh basil leaves, torn (added after broiling)

**Prep Notes:**

- **Cutting the steaks**: Stand the cabbage upright on the cut end and slice straight down through the core with a sharp knife. The core is the only thing holding the outer leaves together — cut through it, not around it. Aim for 1.25-inch thickness; thinner steaks fall apart, thicker ones stay tough.
- **Mozzarella**: Low-moisture part-skim is essential here — fresh mozzarella releases too much water and the steaks are already moist from the sauce. Shredded or torn from a block both work; pre-shredded bags have anti-caking coating that slightly inhibits melting, but it's fine.
- **Sauce quantity**: This recipe makes a full pan of sauce and uses it generously — once under the steam, once over the top before toppings. The excess sauce in the pan doubles as a dipping sauce or can be reserved for another meal.
- **Mushrooms**: Slice thin so they cook through under the broiler without needing to be pre-cooked. Thick mushroom slices won't soften in time.
- **Roasted bell pepper**: Easiest option is jarred roasted red peppers — drain well, pat dry, and slice into strips. To roast fresh: halve the pepper, press flat on a foil-lined pan, broil 10-12 minutes until the skin is blackened, then seal in a bag or covered bowl for 5 minutes to steam, then peel the skin off and slice. Red or orange peppers have more sweetness than green; either works for color.
- **Sun-dried tomatoes**: Oil-packed have better flavor. Blot briefly before chopping to prevent the oil from pooling on top.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Make the sauce.** Heat 0.5 tsp olive oil in a small saucepan over medium heat. Add garlic and cook 60 seconds until fragrant. Add hand-crushed tomatoes, oregano, basil, fennel, red pepper flakes, black pepper, and salt. Simmer 10-12 minutes until slightly thickened. Taste and adjust seasoning — it should be bold. Set aside. (Skip this step if using jarred sauce; just warm it and season aggressively.)

2. **Sear the cabbage.** Pat the cabbage steaks dry. Season both sides with salt, black pepper, and a light dusting of garlic powder. Heat avocado oil in a large oven-safe skillet (cast iron preferred) over medium-high heat until shimmering. Add steaks and press down gently. Sear undisturbed 3-4 minutes until the underside is deep golden-brown. Flip and sear the other side 2-3 minutes.

3. **Steam with sauce.** Reduce heat to medium-low. Spoon a generous amount of sauce over each steak (2-3 tbsp each) and around the pan. Cover tightly with a lid or foil. Steam 7-9 minutes until the steaks are just fork-tender through the thickest part — a knife should meet light resistance, not crunch. Do not overcook; they need to hold together under the toppings.

4. **Cook the chicken.** While the cabbage steams, season chicken strips with Italian seasoning, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Heat 0.5 tsp avocado oil in a small skillet over medium-high. Sear chicken strips 2-3 minutes per side until cooked through and lightly browned. Remove from heat.

5. **Set oven to broil** on HIGH. Position rack 6 inches from the broiler element.

6. **Load the steaks.** Uncover the cabbage. Spoon another generous layer of sauce over each steak — don't be shy, press it into the leaves slightly with the back of the spoon. Distribute the chicken strips evenly across both steaks. Scatter mushroom slices, roasted bell pepper strips, sun-dried tomatoes, banana pepper rings, and olives if using. Finish with an even layer of shredded mozzarella covering everything.

7. **Broil.** Transfer skillet to the oven. Broil 4-6 minutes, watching closely, until the mozzarella is fully melted, bubbly, and has golden-brown spots. The edges of the cabbage may char slightly — this is good.

8. **Serve.** Remove from the oven and let rest 2 minutes. Scatter torn fresh basil over the top. Add red pepper flakes to taste. Eat directly from the pan or transfer carefully with a wide spatula.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~620 total (chicken ~165, mozzarella ~105, cabbage ~115, sauce ~70, mushrooms ~10, sun-dried tomatoes ~25, banana peppers ~10, avocado oil ~60, olives ~30 if using).
- **The core is structural**: Do not cut around the core — it is the only thing holding the steak together under the weight of toppings. If a few outer leaves loosen during cooking, tuck them back under before loading toppings.
- **Searing matters**: The caramelization on the cabbage creates a slightly nutty, savory base that reads more like a crust than a vegetable. Don't skip it or rush it — pale, steamed cabbage underneath loses the whole effect.
- **Leftovers**: The remaining sauce keeps in the fridge 4-5 days. Use it for the spaghetti and meatballs later in the week.
