# Sheet Pan Walleye with Roasted Vegetables

## Ingredients

### Walleye

- 1 large walleye fillet (about 1.5 lbs)
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp lemon pepper seasoning
- 2 tbsp lemon juice
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp dried dill
- 0.5 tsp paprika
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Roasted Vegetables

- 1 lb potatoes, diced into 1-inch cubes
- 3 large carrots, peeled and cut into 2-inch pieces
- 1 pint cherry tomatoes
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- Salt and pepper to taste

### For Serving

- Fresh parsley, chopped
- Lemon wedges

**Prep Notes:**

- Cut potatoes and carrots smaller so they cook at same rate as softer veggies
- Pat walleye completely dry before seasoning
- Cherry tomatoes will burst and create a delicious sauce

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Prep Vegetables**:
   - Preheat oven to 425°F
   - On a large rimmed sheet pan, toss potatoes, carrots, and cherry tomatoes with 2 tbsp olive oil, thyme, garlic powder, salt, and pepper
   - Spread in single layer
   - Roast for 20 minutes

2. **Prepare Walleye**:
   - While vegetables roast, pat walleye fillet dry with paper towels
   - In a small bowl, mix together 2 tbsp olive oil, lemon zest, lemon juice, minced garlic, dill, paprika, salt, and pepper
   - Brush mixture over both sides of walleye

3. **Add Fish and Finish**:
   - Remove sheet pan from oven
   - Push vegetables to sides of pan, creating space in center
   - Add cherry tomatoes to pan among the vegetables
   - Place walleye fillet in center of pan
   - Return to oven and roast 10-12 minutes until fish flakes easily with fork and reaches 145°F internal temperature
   - Walleye is done when it's opaque and flakes apart easily

4. **Serve**:
   - Transfer walleye to serving platter
   - Arrange roasted vegetables around fish
   - Garnish with fresh parsley
   - Serve with lemon wedges for squeezing

## Notes

- Walleye is a mild, flaky white fish with delicate flavor
- Don't overcook walleye - it cooks quickly and can dry out
- Cherry tomatoes add moisture and a pop of sweetness
- The combination of lemon and dill is classic for white fish
- This is a complete one-pan meal with minimal cleanup
- Vegetables get caramelized and delicious from roasting
