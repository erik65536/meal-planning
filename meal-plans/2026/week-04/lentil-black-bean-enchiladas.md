# Rolled Lentil & Black Bean Enchiladas with Cauliflower

## Ingredients

### Enchiladas

- 8-10 low-carb tortillas
- 1.5 cups red enchilada sauce, divided
- 1 cup shredded Mexican cheese blend
- 0.5 cup Greek yogurt (for serving)
- Fresh cilantro (for serving)

### Filling

- 1 cup dried green or brown lentils (or 2.5 cups cooked lentils)
- 1 can (15 oz) black beans, rinsed and drained
- 2 cups cauliflower florets, chopped small
- 1 yellow onion, diced
- 1 red bell pepper, diced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 cup corn (frozen or canned)
- 1 tbsp olive oil

### Seasoning

- 1 tbsp chili powder
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp oregano
- 0.5 tsp smoked paprika
- Salt and pepper to taste

**Prep Notes:**

- Cook lentils ahead of time or use canned lentils for speed
- Warm tortillas before rolling to prevent cracking
- Chop cauliflower small so it incorporates well in filling

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Cook Lentils** (if starting with dried):
   - Rinse lentils and place in pot with 2.5 cups water
   - Bring to boil, reduce to simmer
   - Cook 20-25 minutes until tender but not mushy
   - Drain any excess liquid and set aside

2. **Make Filling**:
   - Preheat oven to 375°F
   - Heat olive oil in large skillet over medium-high heat
   - Add onion and bell pepper, sauté 5 minutes until softened
   - Add chopped cauliflower and cook 5-7 minutes until tender
   - Add garlic and cook 1 minute until fragrant
   - Add cooked lentils, black beans, corn, and all seasonings
   - Stir well and cook 3-4 minutes to combine flavors
   - Remove from heat and let cool slightly

3. **Assemble Enchiladas**:
   - Spray a 9x13 inch baking dish with cooking spray
   - Spread 0.5 cup enchilada sauce on bottom of dish
   - Warm tortillas in microwave for 30 seconds wrapped in damp paper towel (makes them pliable)
   - Take one tortilla and place about 0.5 cup filling down the center
   - Roll up tightly and place seam-side down in baking dish
   - Repeat with remaining tortillas and filling
   - Pour remaining enchilada sauce over rolled enchiladas
   - Sprinkle with shredded cheese

4. **Bake**:
   - Cover dish with foil
   - Bake 20 minutes
   - Remove foil and bake another 10-15 minutes until cheese is melted and bubbly

5. **Serve**:
   - Let cool 5 minutes
   - Serve with dollop of Greek yogurt and fresh cilantro

## Notes

- Lentils and black beans provide excellent plant-based protein
- Cauliflower adds bulk and nutrition without being noticeable
- These freeze well - make extra for meal prep
- Green lentils hold their shape better than red lentils
- Warming tortillas is key to prevent cracking when rolling
- Can substitute salsa verde for red enchilada sauce
