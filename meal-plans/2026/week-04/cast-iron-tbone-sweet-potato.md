# Cast Iron T-Bone Steak with Roasted Sweet Potato & Asparagus

## Ingredients

### Steak

- 2 T-bone steaks (about 1-1.25 inches thick)
- Salt and coarse black pepper
- 1 tbsp avocado oil or olive oil
- 2 cloves garlic, smashed
- 1 tsp thyme
- 1 tsp rosemary

### Roasted Sweet Potato

- 2 large sweet potatoes
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp cinnamon
- Pinch of salt

### Asparagus

- 1 lb asparagus, woody ends removed
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp nutritional yeast
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- Squeeze of lemon juice

**Prep Notes:**

- Let steaks come to room temperature for 20-30 minutes before cooking
- Pat steaks completely dry for best sear
- Peel sweet potatoes or leave skin on for extra fiber

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Roast Sweet Potatoes**:
   - Preheat oven to 425°F
   - Cut sweet potatoes into 1-inch cubes
   - Toss with olive oil, cinnamon, and salt on sheet pan
   - Spread in single layer
   - Roast 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway, until tender and caramelized

2. **Prepare Steaks**:
   - Pat steaks completely dry with paper towels
   - Season generously on both sides with salt and pepper
   - Let rest at room temperature

3. **Cook Steaks**:
   - Heat cast iron skillet over high heat until smoking hot
   - Add oil and swirl to coat
   - Carefully place steaks in pan (they should sizzle immediately)
   - Sear without moving for 3-4 minutes until dark golden crust forms
   - Flip and sear other side 3-4 minutes for medium-rare (adjust time for desired doneness)
   - Add butter, garlic, and herbs to pan in last 2 minutes
   - Tilt pan and baste steaks with melted butter
   - Remove steaks to cutting board and let rest 5-10 minutes

4. **Cook Asparagus**:
   - While steaks rest, wipe out most of the fat from skillet (leave some for flavor)
   - Heat skillet over medium-high heat
   - Add asparagus, olive oil, salt, pepper, and garlic powder
   - Sauté 4-6 minutes, tossing occasionally, until tender-crisp
   - Remove from heat and toss with nutritional yeast
   - Squeeze lemon juice over top

5. **Serve**:
   - Plate T-bone steaks with roasted sweet potatoes and asparagus
   - Drizzle any pan juices from resting steaks over meat

## Notes

- T-bone steaks include both strip and tenderloin sections
- Cast iron retains heat for perfect crust
- Cinnamon brings out natural sweetness of sweet potatoes
- Nutritional yeast adds cheesy, umami flavor to asparagus
- Internal temp guide: 125°F rare, 135°F medium-rare, 145°F medium
- Let steak rest to allow juices to redistribute
