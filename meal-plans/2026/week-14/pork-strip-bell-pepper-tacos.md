# Pork Strip Bell Pepper Tacos

Seared pork strips and charred bell peppers loaded into low-carb tortillas with a garlic-lime-cumin Greek yogurt sauce. Bold, protein-rich, and quick to put together. ~520 calories.

## Ingredients

### Pork and Peppers

- 8 oz pork loin or tenderloin, sliced into 0.25-inch strips
- 2 bell peppers (any color), sliced into thin strips
- 0.5 small red onion, thinly sliced
- 1 tsp avocado oil
- 1 tsp chili powder
- 0.75 tsp cumin
- 0.5 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.5 tsp garlic powder
- 0.25 tsp dried oregano
- 0.25 tsp black pepper
- 0.25 tsp cayenne (adjust to taste)
- Salt to taste

### Garlic-Lime Greek Yogurt Sauce

- 0.33 cup plain nonfat Greek yogurt
- 1 clove garlic, finely minced or grated on a Microplane
- 1 tbsp fresh lime juice (about 0.5 lime)
- 0.5 tsp cumin
- 0.25 tsp chili powder
- 0.25 tsp smoked paprika
- Salt to taste

### Tacos

- 3 low-carb tortilla wraps (8-inch)
- Fresh cilantro, roughly chopped
- Lime wedges for serving
- [Optional] thinly sliced jalapeño

**Prep Notes:**

- Slice pork strips thin and as uniform as possible (about 0.25 inch thick) so they cook fast and evenly. Pork tenderloin is the leanest and most tender option; boneless loin chops also work well — just slice against the grain.
- A very hot pan is essential — the pork and peppers should char slightly at the edges, not steam. If the pan isn't hot enough, everything stews instead. Work in two batches if the pan seems crowded.
- Make the yogurt sauce first so the garlic has time to mellow and the flavors meld while you cook.
- Warm the tortillas directly on a gas burner or dry skillet for 30 seconds per side — this transforms the texture and adds a slight char that makes a meaningful difference.
- The yogurt sauce works as both a spread inside the taco and a dipping sauce on the side.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Make Yogurt Sauce**:
   - Stir together Greek yogurt, minced garlic, lime juice, cumin, chili powder, smoked paprika, and salt. Refrigerate until serving.

2. **Season Pork**:
   - Toss pork strips with chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, garlic powder, oregano, cayenne, black pepper, and salt until evenly coated.

3. **Sear Pork**:
   - Heat avocado oil in a large skillet or cast iron over high heat until shimmering and just starting to smoke.
   - Add pork strips in a single layer — work in two batches if needed. Cook undisturbed 2 minutes until charred on the bottom, then toss and cook 1-2 more minutes until cooked through. Transfer to a plate.

4. **Char Peppers and Onion**:
   - In the same pan over high heat, add bell pepper strips and red onion. Cook undisturbed 3 minutes, then toss and cook another 2-3 minutes until charred at the edges and slightly softened. Season with salt.

5. **Warm Tortillas**:
   - Warm each tortilla in a dry skillet or directly over a gas burner, about 30 seconds per side.

6. **Assemble Tacos**:
   - Spread yogurt sauce inside each warm tortilla. Layer with pork strips, charred peppers and onion, fresh cilantro, and jalapeño slices if using.
   - Serve with lime wedges for squeezing.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~520 total (pork ~230 + peppers and onion ~50 + tortillas ~180 + yogurt sauce ~35 + oil ~25) — varies by tortilla brand.
- Low-carb tortillas range widely in calorie content (50-120+ cal each) — check your package.
- The yogurt sauce adds creaminess and tang without the calories of sour cream. A small amount of lime zest stirred in takes it further.
- Leftover pork and pepper mix reheats well in a dry skillet over medium heat; avoid microwaving or the peppers turn soggy.
