# Stuffed Cabbage Roll Casserole

A three-day batch — seasoned cubed steak and rice rolled into boiled cabbage leaves, nestled in spaghetti sauce, and oven-roasted until tender and deeply sauced. Warm spices (cinnamon, allspice) and fresh mint give it a Middle Eastern and Eastern European character. ~480 calories per serving (3 rolls).

## Ingredients

*(Makes 9 rolls / 3 servings — 3 rolls per day)*

### Filling

- 1 lb cubed steak, cut into 0.5-inch pieces
- 0.5 cup long grain white rice, dry (par-cooked before mixing — see prep notes)
- 1 small yellow onion, finely diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 0.33 cup fresh mint leaves, roughly chopped
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 0.5 tsp cumin
- 0.5 tsp ground cinnamon
- 0.25 tsp ground allspice
- 0.5 tsp black pepper
- 0.25 tsp red pepper flakes
- Salt to taste
- 1 tsp avocado oil

### Cabbage

- 1 medium head green cabbage (~2 lbs) — 9 large outer leaves for rolls, inner cabbage reserved

### Sauce

- 1 jar (24 oz) spaghetti sauce (or marinara)
- 0.25 cup low-sodium beef broth
- 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
- 0.5 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.5 tsp garlic powder

**Prep Notes:**

- Cubed steak is mechanically tenderized beef (usually top or bottom round). Cut the already-tenderized pieces into 0.5-inch chunks — they will soften further as they braise in the tomato sauce in the oven.
- Par-cook the rice before mixing into the filling: bring 0.5 cup rice and 0.75 cup water to a boil, cover, and cook only 8 minutes. The rice should be undercooked and still firm — it finishes cooking inside the rolls and absorbs the sauce, so fully cooked rice turns to mush.
- To prepare cabbage leaves: cut around the core of the head with a paring knife. Submerge the whole head in a large pot of boiling salted water. As outer leaves loosen (2-3 minutes), peel them away with tongs and transfer to a cutting board. Repeat until you have 9 large, pliable leaves.
- Trim the thick central rib of each leaf with a knife so the leaf lies flat and rolls without tearing. Keep the rib trim shallow — just shave it down, don't cut through the leaf.
- To roll: place 2-3 tbsp filling near the base of the leaf. Fold the two sides in, then roll snugly from bottom to top like a burrito. Rolls should be firm enough to hold their shape but not so tight the cabbage tears.
- Place rolls seam-side down in the baking dish so they stay closed during roasting.
- Cover tightly with foil for the first 45 minutes to trap steam and prevent the sauce from drying out. Uncover for the last 15 minutes to concentrate and caramelize the sauce.
- Store leftover rolls covered in the baking dish. Reheat at 325°F for 15-20 minutes, or microwave covered with a damp paper towel.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Par-Cook Rice**:
   - Bring 0.75 cup water to a boil. Add rice and a pinch of salt. Cover and cook 8 minutes. Remove from heat and spread on a plate to stop cooking and cool.

2. **Prepare Cabbage Leaves**:
   - Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cut around the core with a paring knife.
   - Submerge the whole head. As outer leaves loosen (2-3 minutes), peel off with tongs and transfer to a colander. Repeat until you have 9 large leaves. Pat dry.
   - Trim the thick center rib of each leaf so it lies flat.

3. **Make Filling**:
   - Heat avocado oil in a skillet over medium-high. Add onion and cook 3-4 minutes until softened. Add garlic and cook 1 minute more.
   - Add cubed steak pieces and cook, stirring occasionally, just until the outside is no longer pink (2-3 minutes — it will finish in the oven).
   - Remove from heat. Stir in par-cooked rice, mint, smoked paprika, oregano, cumin, cinnamon, allspice, black pepper, red pepper flakes, and salt. Taste and adjust seasoning aggressively — the filling should be well-seasoned before it goes into the rolls.

4. **Make Sauce**:
   - Stir together spaghetti sauce, beef broth, Worcestershire sauce, smoked paprika, and garlic powder.
   - Pour about 0.5 cup sauce into the bottom of a 9×13 baking dish to coat.

5. **Roll and Arrange**:
   - Preheat oven to 350°F.
   - Place 2-3 tbsp filling near the base of each cabbage leaf. Fold sides in, roll snugly from bottom to top, and place seam-side down in the baking dish.
   - Repeat for all 9 rolls, arranging them in a single snug layer.
   - Pour remaining sauce over and around the rolls, covering them completely.

6. **Roast**:
   - Cover dish tightly with foil. Bake 45 minutes.
   - Uncover and bake 15-20 minutes more until sauce is bubbling, slightly reduced, and the tops of the rolls have taken on some color.
   - Rest 10 minutes before serving.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~440 per serving (3 rolls): beef ~220 + rice ~75 + cabbage ~30 + sauce ~85 + oil + aromatics ~30.
- The cinnamon-allspice-mint combination is inspired by Middle Eastern and Balkan stuffed cabbage traditions — it lifts the dish well beyond plain beef-tomato flavor.
- The inner cabbage core and smaller leaves can be shredded raw for a quick slaw, sautéed with garlic as a side, or used in soup. Don't throw it out.
- These rolls freeze well individually after baking — wrap each roll and store. Reheat from frozen at 350°F for 25-30 minutes.
