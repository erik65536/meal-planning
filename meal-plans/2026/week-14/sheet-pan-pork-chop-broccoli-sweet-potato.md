# Sheet Pan Pork Chop with Broccoli and Sweet Potato

A chipotle-lime pork chop roasted alongside caramelized sweet potato cubes and charred broccoli florets. Smoky, slightly sweet, with a bright lime finish. ~490 calories.

## Ingredients

### Pork Chop

- 1 boneless pork chop (6-7 oz)
- 1 tsp avocado oil
- 0.75 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.5 tsp chipotle chili powder (or regular chili powder + a pinch of cayenne)
- 0.5 tsp garlic powder
- 0.5 tsp cumin
- 0.25 tsp onion powder
- 0.25 tsp black pepper
- Salt to taste

### Vegetables

- 1 medium sweet potato (~8 oz), cut into 0.75-inch cubes
- 8 oz broccoli florets, cut into medium pieces
- 1.5 tsp avocado oil
- 0.5 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.5 tsp garlic powder
- 0.25 tsp cumin
- Salt and black pepper to taste

### Finishing

- Juice of 0.5 lime
- 1 tbsp fresh cilantro, roughly chopped [Optional]

**Prep Notes:**

- Sweet potato cubes need a 15-minute head start before the pork and broccoli go in — they take much longer to roast. Cut them into uniform 0.75-inch cubes so they cook evenly.
- Pat the pork chop completely dry before applying the rub — a wet surface steams instead of forming a crust. Press the spice blend firmly into both sides.
- Broccoli and pork go in together for the last 20-22 minutes — this way everything finishes simultaneously. Don't add the broccoli earlier or it will be overdone.
- Use avocado oil throughout — 425°F is above olive oil's smoke point.
- Let the pork chop rest 5 minutes after pulling from the oven. The internal temp will carry from 140°F to 145°F during rest.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Preheat**: Oven to 425°F. Line a large rimmed baking sheet with parchment.

2. **Start Sweet Potato**:
   - Toss sweet potato cubes with 1 tsp avocado oil, smoked paprika, garlic powder, cumin, salt, and pepper.
   - Spread in a single layer on the baking sheet. Roast 15 minutes.

3. **Prepare Pork and Broccoli**:
   - While the sweet potato roasts, mix all pork spices. Pat chop completely dry and rub with 1 tsp avocado oil, then press the spice blend into both sides.
   - Toss broccoli florets with 0.5 tsp avocado oil, a pinch of garlic powder, salt, and pepper.

4. **Add Pork and Broccoli**:
   - After 15 minutes, flip the sweet potato cubes and push them to one side of the pan.
   - Add broccoli florets and nestle the pork chop onto the pan.
   - Roast another 20-22 minutes until pork reaches 140-145°F and the broccoli edges are charred and crispy.

5. **Rest and Finish**:
   - Remove pork chop and rest on a cutting board for 5 minutes (temp carries to 145°F while resting).
   - Squeeze lime juice over the vegetables while still hot.
   - Slice or plate the chop whole alongside the vegetables. Scatter cilantro if using.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~470 total (pork ~200 + sweet potato ~100 + broccoli ~55 + oil ~110 + lime ~5).
- Chipotle chili powder gives smokiness with mild heat — if you prefer more heat, add 0.25 tsp cayenne to the pork rub.
- The sweet potato caramelizes significantly at 425°F — the cut faces will deepen to a dark amber. Don't pull them early; that color is the flavor.
- The lime juice squeezed hot over the roasted vegetables brightens the whole plate and cuts through the smokiness.
