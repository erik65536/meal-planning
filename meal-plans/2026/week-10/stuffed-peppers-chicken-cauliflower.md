# Stuffed Bell Peppers with Spiced Chicken, Cauliflower Rice & Black Beans

Two large bell peppers stuffed with boldly seasoned shredded chicken breast, cauliflower rice, black beans, and wilted spinach in a smoky tomato base. No cheese — the spice blend and tomatoes carry all the flavor. ~310 calories.

## Ingredients

### Peppers

- 2 large bell peppers (red, orange, or yellow — sweetest flavor)
- 0.25 cup water (for the baking dish)

### Filling

- 1 boneless, skinless chicken breast (6 oz), poached and shredded
- 1 tsp avocado oil
- 0.5 small yellow onion, finely diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1.5 cups cauliflower rice (fresh or frozen, thawed)
- 0.5 cup canned black beans, drained and rinsed (use remainder from taco salad night)
- 0.5 cup canned diced tomatoes, drained
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 2 cups fresh spinach, roughly chopped
- 2 tsp cumin
- 1.5 tsp chili powder
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.5 tsp dried oregano
- 0.5 tsp garlic powder
- 0.25 tsp cayenne
- Salt & Black Pepper

### For Serving

- Fresh cilantro or flat-leaf parsley, roughly chopped
- Hot sauce (Cholula, Tapatio, or similar)
- Lime wedge
- 2 tbsp plain Greek yogurt (optional, in place of sour cream)

**Prep Notes:**

- Cut the tops off the peppers and remove seeds and membranes. Dice the edible parts of the pepper tops and add them directly into the filling — zero waste.
- If using frozen cauliflower rice, thaw and squeeze out excess moisture in a clean towel first. Wet cauli rice will make the filling soggy.
- Poach the chicken breast before starting everything else: simmer in water or broth for 13-15 minutes, then shred with two forks while still warm.
- Bloom the tomato paste in the pan for 1-2 minutes before adding liquid — this deepens the flavor significantly.
- The filling can be made ahead and refrigerated; stuff and bake when ready.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Preheat**: Oven to 375°F. Arrange hollowed bell peppers upright in a baking dish. Pour 0.25 cup water into the bottom of the dish (creates steam and keeps peppers from drying out).

2. **Cook Chicken & Aromatics**:
   - Heat avocado oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
   - Add diced onion (including diced pepper tops) and cook 4-5 minutes until softened.
   - Add garlic and cook 1 minute until fragrant.
   - Add shredded chicken. Toss to combine and cook 2-3 minutes, letting some edges crisp slightly. Season with salt and pepper.

3. **Build the Filling**:
   - Push chicken to one side. Add tomato paste to the empty side of the pan. Cook tomato paste, stirring, for 2 minutes until it darkens slightly.
   - Add cumin, chili powder, smoked paprika, oregano, garlic powder, and cayenne. Stir into the tomato paste and cook 1 minute until fragrant.
   - Mix everything together. Add cauliflower rice, black beans, and diced tomatoes. Stir to combine.
   - Add spinach. Stir and cook until wilted, about 2 minutes.
   - Taste and adjust seasoning — the filling should be bold and well-seasoned.

4. **Stuff & Bake**:
   - Spoon the filling generously into each pepper, packing it in and mounding it above the rim.
   - Cover the baking dish tightly with foil.
   - Bake for 30 minutes until peppers are tender when pierced with a fork.
   - Uncover and bake another 5-8 minutes to concentrate the filling slightly.

5. **Serve**:
   - Plate peppers on a wide plate.
   - Dollop Greek yogurt alongside if using.
   - Scatter fresh cilantro or parsley over the top. Squeeze a lime wedge over everything.
   - Serve with hot sauce on the side.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~310 total (chicken ~140 + peppers ~50 + cauliflower rice ~25 + black beans ~50 + tomatoes/spinach ~30 + oil ~15).
- **No Cheese**: The smoked paprika, tomato paste, and well-seasoned chicken do the work that cheese would otherwise do. The filling won't feel like it's missing anything.
- **Cauliflower Rice vs. Regular Rice**: Swapping regular rice for cauliflower rice saves about 100 calories per serving and adds volume. The texture blends seamlessly into the filling.
- **Black Beans**: Use the rest of the can opened for the taco salad bowl earlier this week.
- **Leftovers**: Stuffed peppers reheat very well. Store any extra in the fridge for 3-4 days.
