# Roasted Carrot-Ginger-Butternut Squash Soup with Lemon-Herb Chicken Breast

Golden, silky pureed soup with deep warmth from ginger, turmeric, and cumin. Paired with a simple baked lemon-herb chicken breast for protein. Make a double batch of soup today — it reheats for the second soup dinner this week. ~385 calories.

## Ingredients

### Soup (Double Batch — Makes 2 Servings)

- 2 cups butternut squash, peeled and cut into 1-inch cubes
- 3 large carrots, peeled and roughly chopped (about 2.5 cups)
- 1 medium yellow onion, roughly chopped
- 4 cloves garlic, smashed
- 1 tbsp avocado oil
- 3 cups low-sodium chicken broth
- 0.5 cup light coconut milk
- 2-inch knob fresh ginger, peeled and roughly chopped
- 2 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.5 tsp coriander
- 0.5 tsp cayenne (adjust to taste)
- Salt & Black Pepper

### Lemon-Herb Chicken Breast

- 1 boneless, skinless chicken breast (5-6 oz)
- 1 tsp olive oil
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 0.5 tsp garlic powder
- 0.5 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.25 tsp onion powder
- Zest of 0.5 lemon
- Salt & Black Pepper

### For Serving (Per Bowl)

- 1 tbsp plain Greek yogurt, dolloped on top
- Fresh cilantro or flat-leaf parsley, chopped
- Pinch of red pepper flakes
- Lemon wedge

**Prep Notes:**

- Make a double batch of soup today. Refrigerate the second portion for the shrimp soup night later this week — reheat and serve with garlic shrimp.
- Roasting the squash and carrots at high heat before blending is the key to a deep, sweet, caramelized flavor. Don't skip the roast.
- The chicken breast can bake on a second rack while the vegetables roast — time them to finish together.
- An immersion blender is easiest for blending directly in the pot. A countertop blender gives a silkier result; cool the soup slightly and vent the lid before blending.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Preheat**: Oven to 425°F. Line a large baking sheet with parchment paper.

2. **Roast Vegetables**:
   - Toss butternut squash, carrots, onion, and garlic with avocado oil, salt, and pepper.
   - Spread in a single layer on the baking sheet.
   - Roast 25-30 minutes, flipping once halfway through, until caramelized and tender.

3. **Bake Chicken**:
   - Pat chicken breast dry. Rub all over with olive oil, then coat with oregano, garlic powder, smoked paprika, onion powder, lemon zest, salt, and pepper.
   - Place in a small baking dish or on a foil-lined baking sheet.
   - Bake at 375°F (or use the lower rack at 425°F and reduce time) for 20-25 minutes until internal temp reaches 165°F.
   - Let rest 5 minutes before slicing.

4. **Build Soup**:
   - Transfer roasted vegetables to a large saucepan. Add chicken broth and fresh ginger.
   - Add cumin, turmeric, smoked paprika, coriander, cayenne, salt, and pepper.
   - Bring to a simmer and cook 5 minutes.
   - Remove from heat. Blend with an immersion blender until completely smooth.
   - Stir in light coconut milk. Return to low heat and simmer 2-3 minutes. Taste and adjust seasoning.
   - Add a squeeze of lemon to brighten. Divide into two portions; refrigerate the second portion for later in the week.

5. **Serve**:
   - Ladle one serving of soup into a large bowl.
   - Dollop Greek yogurt in the center and dust with red pepper flakes.
   - Slice chicken breast and serve alongside the soup bowl.
   - Garnish soup with fresh cilantro or parsley and a lemon wedge.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~385 total (soup ~245 + chicken breast ~130 + yogurt garnish ~10).
- **Double Batch Logic**: The soup takes the same effort to make 1 serving vs. 2. Make both now; the second serving keeps 4-5 days in the fridge. Reheat and pair with garlic shrimp later this week.
- **Soup Consistency**: After blending, if the soup feels too thick, add broth 0.25 cup at a time until it reaches a consistency you like.
- **Chicken**: The lemon zest in the dry rub is essential — it lifts the flavor and complements the ginger and turmeric in the soup.
