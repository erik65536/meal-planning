# Roasted Carrot-Ginger-Butternut Squash Soup with Garlic-Lime Sautéed Shrimp

The second serving of the soup made earlier this week, reheated and paired with quick garlic-lime shrimp. The shrimp cook in under 5 minutes, making this the fastest dinner of the week. ~355 calories.

## Ingredients

### Soup (Reheated from Earlier This Week)

- 1 serving carrot-ginger-butternut squash soup (already made and refrigerated)

### Garlic-Lime Sautéed Shrimp

- 6 oz raw shrimp, peeled and deveined (large, 21-25 count)
- 1 tsp olive oil
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 0.5 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.25 tsp cumin
- 0.25 tsp red pepper flakes
- Zest and juice of 0.5 lime
- Salt & Black Pepper
- Fresh cilantro, roughly chopped

### For Serving

- 1 tbsp plain Greek yogurt, dolloped on soup
- Extra lime wedge
- Pinch of red pepper flakes

**Prep Notes:**

- Pat shrimp completely dry before cooking — moisture is the enemy of a good sear. Any water in the pan will steam instead of sear.
- Shrimp go from raw to overcooked in under 60 seconds past done. Watch carefully: they're done when they curl into a loose "C" and turn fully opaque. A tight curl means overcooked.
- Season the soup to taste when reheating — it may need a pinch more salt or a squeeze of lemon after sitting in the fridge.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Reheat Soup**:
   - Transfer soup from the fridge to a small saucepan. Heat over medium-low, stirring occasionally, until steaming. Taste and adjust salt, pepper, and lemon as needed.
   - Ladle into a large bowl and keep warm.

2. **Cook Shrimp**:
   - Pat shrimp dry with paper towels. Season with smoked paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper.
   - Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering.
   - Add shrimp in a single layer. Do not stir. Cook 60-90 seconds until pink on the bottom.
   - Flip each shrimp. Add minced garlic and red pepper flakes to the pan. Cook 60 seconds more, tossing garlic to prevent burning.
   - Remove from heat. Add lime zest and squeeze lime juice over the pan. Toss to coat.

3. **Serve**:
   - Dollop Greek yogurt in the center of the soup bowl and dust with red pepper flakes.
   - Arrange garlic-lime shrimp alongside the soup bowl (or lay them on top of the soup if preferred).
   - Garnish with fresh cilantro and extra lime wedge.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~355 total (soup ~245 + shrimp ~100 + yogurt ~10).
- **Quick Night**: The soup is already done. Total active cooking time is under 10 minutes — just reheat and cook shrimp.
- **Shrimp on the Soup**: You can lay the garlic-lime shrimp directly over the soup as a topper. The broth mingles with the shrimp pan drippings for even more flavor.
- **Shrimp Size**: Large shrimp (21-25 count per pound) are the right size here — they stay juicy, don't overcook as easily, and have enough body alongside the soup.
