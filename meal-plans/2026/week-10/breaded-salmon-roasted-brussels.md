# Breaded Salmon with Crispy Roasted Brussels Sprouts

Pre-seasoned, pre-breaded salmon fillets baked alongside crispy, caramelized Brussels sprouts with garlic and a dijon-lemon finish, plus a simple herb-cucumber salad. Light, filling, and deeply savory at roughly 390 calories.

## Ingredients

### Salmon

- 1 pre-seasoned, pre-breaded salmon fillets (275 cal total per package)
- Cooking spray or parchment paper

### Crispy Roasted Brussels Sprouts

- 12 oz Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved through the stem
- 1 tsp olive oil
- 3 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
- 0.25 tsp red pepper flakes
- 0.25 tsp garlic powder
- Salt & Black Pepper
- 1 tsp dijon mustard
- Juice of 0.5 lemon

### Cucumber-Herb Salad

- 1 medium cucumber, thinly sliced into rounds
- 0.25 red onion, thinly sliced
- 2 tbsp fresh parsley or dill, roughly chopped
- 1 tsp olive oil
- 1 tbsp red wine vinegar or lemon juice
- Salt & Black Pepper
- Pinch of red pepper flakes

**Prep Notes:**

- Halve Brussels sprouts through the stem so the cut face lies flat on the pan — this is where the caramelization happens. Don't skip the flat-side-down placement.
- Dry the sprouts thoroughly before tossing with oil; moisture prevents browning.
- The dijon-lemon finish goes on after roasting, not before — it would burn in the oven.
- The cucumber salad can be made ahead and refrigerated while everything else cooks.
- Follow package instructions for salmon temperature and time; typically 375-400°F for 18-22 minutes.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Preheat**: Oven to 400°F. Line two baking sheets with parchment paper.

2. **Make Cucumber Salad**:
   - Toss cucumber rounds and red onion with olive oil, vinegar, parsley, salt, pepper, and a pinch of red pepper flakes.
   - Let sit at room temperature while everything else cooks — the acid will slightly pickle the cucumbers and mellow the onion.

3. **Roast Brussels Sprouts**:
   - Toss halved Brussels sprouts with olive oil, garlic powder, red pepper flakes, salt, and pepper.
   - Arrange cut-side down in a single layer on one baking sheet. Scatter sliced garlic over the top.
   - Roast at 425°F for 20-22 minutes until the cut faces are deep golden brown and the outer leaves are crispy.

4. **Cook Salmon**:
   - Place salmon fillets on the second lined baking sheet.
   - Cook according to package instructions (typically 375-400°F for 18-22 minutes until golden and cooked through).
   - Start salmon partway through the Brussels sprouts cook time so they finish together.

5. **Finish & Serve**:
   - Remove Brussels sprouts from oven. Whisk dijon mustard with lemon juice and drizzle over the sprouts. Toss to coat.
   - Plate salmon alongside Brussels sprouts and cucumber salad.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~390 total (salmon 275 + Brussels sprouts ~75 + cucumber salad ~40).
- **Brussels Sprouts**: High in fiber, vitamin C, and vitamin K. At 12 oz, they roast down significantly in volume but still provide great bulk and staying power.
- **Dijon Finish**: The mustard-lemon drizzle adds sharp, tangy flavor without any significant calories — it makes plain roasted sprouts taste like a restaurant side.
- **Cucumber Salad**: Functions as a fresh, bright acid contrast to the savory salmon and sprouts. No dressing needed beyond oil and vinegar.
