# Lamb Steak with Za'atar Rub and Roasted Asparagus and Red Onion

## Ingredients

### Lamb Steak

- 1 lamb leg steak or shoulder steak (7-8 oz), ~600 cal
- 1 tsp avocado oil
- 1.5 tsp za'atar (store-bought or see note below)
- 0.5 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.5 tsp garlic powder
- 0.25 tsp ground cumin
- 0.25 tsp ground coriander
- 0.25 tsp black pepper
- 0.5 tsp salt

### Roasted Asparagus & Red Onion

- 1 bunch asparagus (about 10-12 spears), woody ends snapped off
- 0.5 medium red onion, cut into 0.75-inch wedges through the root
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 3 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
- 0.5 tsp sumac (or substitute with a squeeze of pomegranate molasses at the end)
- 0.5 tsp dried thyme
- Salt and black pepper to taste

### Finishing

- 0.25 cup plain Greek yogurt (2%), for serving
- 1 tbsp fresh mint, roughly torn
- 1 tsp pomegranate molasses, drizzled over yogurt (optional)

**Prep Notes:**

- Let the lamb sit at room temperature 20-30 minutes before grilling — cold meat from the refrigerator grills unevenly and produces a gray band of overcooked meat under the crust.
- Pat the steak dry before applying the rub — moisture steams the surface and prevents a crust from forming.
- Za'atar already contains sesame seeds and thyme; if yours is heavy on sesame you can reduce the dried thyme slightly.
- Keep red onion wedges intact through the root so they hold together on the pan and caramelize rather than falling apart into rings.
- Asparagus roasts fast — add it partway through the red onion's cook time so they finish together.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Preheat & Prep**:
   - Preheat oven to 425°F for roasted vegetables. Preheat an outdoor grill or cast iron grill pan to medium-high.
   - Line a baking sheet with parchment.

2. **Season Lamb**:
   - Pat steak dry. Rub avocado oil all over, then press za'atar, smoked paprika, garlic powder, cumin, coriander, salt, and pepper into both sides. Set aside at room temperature while you start the vegetables.

3. **Roast Red Onion**:
   - Toss red onion wedges with 0.5 tbsp olive oil, garlic slices, sumac, salt, and pepper. Spread on baking sheet.
   - Roast 12 minutes.

4. **Add Asparagus**:
   - Toss asparagus spears with remaining 0.5 tbsp olive oil, dried thyme, salt, and pepper.
   - Add to the baking sheet alongside the onions. Roast another 12-14 minutes until asparagus is tender with crispy tips and onion edges are caramelized.

5. **Grill Lamb**:
   - Grill lamb steak over medium-high heat 4-5 minutes per side for medium (145°F internal) or 3-4 minutes per side for medium-rare (135°F internal).
   - Let rest 5 minutes before slicing — resting allows juices to redistribute and keeps the steak from bleeding out when cut.

6. **Serve**:
   - Plate asparagus and red onion alongside sliced lamb.
   - Add a spoonful of Greek yogurt on the side. Drizzle pomegranate molasses over the yogurt if using. Finish with torn fresh mint.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~730 total (lamb ~600 + asparagus ~40 + red onion ~30 + olive oil ~60 + Greek yogurt ~25).
- **Za'atar blend** (if making your own): 1 tbsp dried thyme, 1 tsp dried oregano, 1 tsp sumac, 0.5 tsp sesame seeds, 0.25 tsp salt — mix and store in a jar.
- Lamb leg steaks are leaner than shoulder steaks; shoulder has more marbling and is more forgiving if slightly overcooked.
