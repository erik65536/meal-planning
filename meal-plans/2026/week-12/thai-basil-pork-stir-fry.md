# Thai Basil Pork Stir Fry

## Ingredients

### Pork

- 1 boneless pork chop (6 oz), cut into 0.75-inch cubes, ~180 cal
- 1 tsp avocado oil
- 0.5 tsp white pepper
- 0.5 tsp garlic powder

### Thai Basil Sauce

- 1.5 tbsp oyster sauce
- 1 tbsp fish sauce
- 1 tsp low-sodium soy sauce
- 1 tsp sugar (or honey)
- 1 tsp rice vinegar
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2-3 Thai chilies or 1 serrano, thinly sliced (adjust to heat preference)
- 1 tsp chili garlic sauce (sambal oelek or similar)

### Stir Fry

- 1 bag (12 oz) frozen stir fry vegetables (broccoli, snap peas, carrots, water chestnuts mix), ~180 cal

### Finishing

- 0.75 cup fresh basil leaves (Thai basil preferred; regular Italian basil works)
- 1 tsp lime juice, squeezed at the end
- Sliced fresh chili for garnish (optional)

**Prep Notes:**

- Fish sauce has a pungent aroma raw but mellows and deepens dramatically once it hits a hot pan — don't adjust quantity based on the smell of the bottle.
- Combine all sauce ingredients in a bowl before cooking — this moves quickly once the pan is hot.
- Fresh basil goes in at the very end, off the heat. The residual warmth wilts it gently without turning it black and bitter.
- White pepper gives a different heat character than black pepper — it's sharper and more floral, which suits this Thai profile well.
- As with the other stir fry this week: cook pork separately first, then build the stir fry. Crowding steams instead of sears.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Make Sauce**:
   - Whisk together oyster sauce, fish sauce, soy sauce, sugar, rice vinegar, garlic, fresh chilies, and chili garlic sauce in a small bowl. Set aside.

2. **Sear Pork**:
   - Pat pork cubes dry. Season with white pepper and garlic powder.
   - Heat avocado oil in a large skillet or wok over high heat until just smoking.
   - Add pork in a single layer. Sear undisturbed 90 seconds, flip, cook another 60-90 seconds until browned and cooked through (145°F internal). Remove and set aside.

3. **Cook Vegetables**:
   - In the same pan over high heat, add frozen stir fry vegetables directly from the bag. Spread in a single layer.
   - Cook undisturbed 2-3 minutes, then stir and cook another 2-3 minutes until tender-crisp with some char.

4. **Combine & Glaze**:
   - Return pork to the pan. Pour Thai basil sauce over everything. Toss to coat.
   - Cook 60-90 seconds, stirring constantly, until the sauce reduces and clings to the pork and vegetables.

5. **Finish & Serve**:
   - Remove from heat. Add fresh basil leaves and squeeze of lime juice. Toss to wilt the basil.
   - Plate immediately and garnish with fresh sliced chili if desired.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~490 total (pork ~180 + vegetables ~180 + oyster/fish sauce ~60 + oil ~40 + sugar ~10 + basil ~5).
- Thai basil has a slightly anise-like flavor that works better here; if using Italian basil add a tiny pinch of fennel seed to the sauce.
- Fish sauce is high in sodium — if watching salt intake, reduce fish sauce to 0.5 tbsp and increase soy sauce slightly.
