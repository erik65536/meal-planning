# Pork Gochujang Stir Fry

Cubed pork chop seared hot and glazed in a bold Korean-inspired gochujang sauce with frozen stir fry vegetables. Deep heat, savory depth, and a subtle sweetness. ~480 calories.

## Ingredients

### Pork

- 1 boneless pork chop (6 oz), cut into 0.75-inch cubes, ~180 cal
- 1 tsp avocado oil
- 0.5 tsp garlic powder
- 0.5 tsp black pepper

### Gochujang Sauce

- 1.5 tbsp gochujang paste
- 1 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
- 1 tsp rice vinegar
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp honey or brown sugar
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated (or 0.5 tsp ground)

### Stir Fry

- 1 bag (12 oz) frozen stir fry vegetables (broccoli, snap peas, carrots, water chestnuts mix), ~180 cal
- 0.5 tsp red pepper flakes (optional for extra heat)

### Finishing

- 2 green onions, thinly sliced
- 1 tsp sesame seeds
- 1 tbsp fresh cilantro, roughly chopped

**Prep Notes:**

- Mix the gochujang sauce ingredients in a small bowl before you start cooking — everything moves fast in a hot pan and you won't have time to measure mid-cook.
- Pat pork cubes dry before seasoning — surface moisture causes steaming instead of searing and prevents browning.
- Gochujang burns quickly at high heat; add the sauce only after the pork is back in the pan and the heat is slightly reduced, then let it caramelize for 60-90 seconds rather than longer.
- Frozen vegetables go in straight from the bag — thawing first makes them mushy.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Make Sauce**:
   - Whisk together gochujang, soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, honey, garlic, and ginger in a small bowl. Set aside.

2. **Sear Pork**:
   - Pat pork cubes dry. Season with garlic powder and black pepper.
   - Heat avocado oil in a large skillet or wok over high heat until just smoking.
   - Add pork in a single layer. Sear undisturbed 90 seconds, flip, cook another 60-90 seconds until browned and cooked through (145°F internal). Remove and set aside.

3. **Cook Vegetables**:
   - In the same pan over high heat, add frozen stir fry vegetables directly from the bag. Spread in a single layer.
   - Cook undisturbed 2-3 minutes, then stir and cook another 2-3 minutes until tender-crisp with some char spots.

4. **Combine & Glaze**:
   - Return pork to the pan. Pour gochujang sauce over everything. Toss to coat.
   - Cook 60-90 seconds, stirring constantly, until the sauce thickens and clings. Add red pepper flakes if using.

5. **Serve**:
   - Plate and top with green onions, sesame seeds, and fresh cilantro.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~480 total (pork ~180 + vegetables ~180 + gochujang sauce ~80 + oil ~40).
- Gochujang heat level varies by brand — start with 1 tbsp if you want milder heat, go up to 2 tbsp for extra spicy.
