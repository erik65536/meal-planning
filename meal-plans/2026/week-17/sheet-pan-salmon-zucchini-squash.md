# Sheet Pan Salmon with Zucchini & Yellow Squash

Skin-on salmon roasted over lemon-herb zucchini and yellow squash coins — simple, quick, and clean. The vegetables go in first to get a head start; the salmon finishes on top with crispy skin. ~360 calories.

## Ingredients

### Salmon

- 1 skin-on salmon fillet (~6 oz), thawed
- 1 tsp olive oil
- 0.5 tsp garlic powder
- 0.5 tsp dried dill
- Zest of 0.5 lemon
- Salt and black pepper to taste

### Vegetables

- 1 medium zucchini, cut into 0.5-inch coins
- 1 medium yellow squash, cut into 0.5-inch coins
- 1 tsp olive oil
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- 0.5 tsp dried Italian herbs or dried oregano
- Salt and black pepper to taste

### For Serving

- Lemon wedge
- Fresh dill or parsley [Optional]

**Prep Notes:**

- Thaw salmon overnight in the refrigerator, or quick-thaw: leave in packaging and submerge in a bowl of cold water for 30–45 minutes, changing water once.
- Pat the salmon completely dry — especially the skin side — before seasoning. Moisture is the enemy of crispy skin.
- Cut the coins roughly the same thickness so they roast evenly. If the squash is very wide, cut coins in half into half-moons.
- Don't overlap the squash on the pan; they need direct heat to roast rather than steam.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Thaw and preheat.** Thaw salmon fully and pat dry. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a sheet pan with foil or parchment.

2. **Season the vegetables.** Toss zucchini and yellow squash with olive oil, minced garlic, Italian herbs, salt, and pepper. Spread in a single layer on the sheet pan — do not overlap.

3. **Roast the vegetables first.** Roast 10–12 minutes until beginning to soften and edges are starting to brown.

4. **Season the salmon.** While the vegetables roast, rub the flesh side of the salmon with olive oil. Mix garlic powder, dill, lemon zest, salt, and pepper and press onto the flesh side. Leave the skin side bare.

5. **Add the salmon.** Push the vegetables to the edges of the pan. Place salmon skin-side down in the center.

6. **Finish roasting.** Return pan to the oven and roast 10–13 minutes until the salmon flakes easily at the thickest point and reads 125–130°F internal for medium, or 140°F for well done. The skin should be visibly crisped.

7. **Serve.** Plate salmon alongside the squash. Squeeze the lemon wedge over everything. Scatter fresh dill or parsley if using.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~360 total (salmon ~240, zucchini + squash ~45, olive oil ~75).
- **Skin**: eat it — it crisps well at 425°F and contains the highest concentration of omega-3s. Use a thin spatula to lift; properly crisped skin releases cleanly from parchment or foil.
- **Doneness**: salmon at 125–130°F is medium with a slightly translucent center. It will continue to cook off the heat — pull it when it just flakes at the thickest part.
- **Watery squash**: if the coins look like they're steaming rather than roasting, increase oven to 450°F for the last 5 minutes to drive off moisture.
