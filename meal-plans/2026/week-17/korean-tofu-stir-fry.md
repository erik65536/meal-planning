# Korean Tofu Stir-Fry with Snap Peas, Mushrooms & Cabbage

Crispy seared tofu and a heap of vegetables — snap peas, mushrooms, bell pepper, carrots, and shredded cabbage — tossed in a bold gochujang sauce. Makes 3 servings — cook once, eat three times. Serve over steamed white rice. ~420 calories per serving.

## Ingredients

### Tofu

- 1 block (14–16 oz) extra-firm tofu, pressed and cut into 1-inch cubes
- 0.5 tbsp avocado oil

### Vegetables

- 2 cups snap peas, trimmed
- 1 bell pepper, thinly sliced
- 8 oz cremini or shiitake mushrooms, sliced
- 2 medium carrots, sliced thin on the diagonal
- 2–3 cups green cabbage, thinly shredded
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
- 3 green onions, sliced (whites and greens separated)
- 0.5 tbsp avocado oil

### Korean Sauce

- 2.5 tbsp gochujang
- 2.5 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
- 1.5 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tbsp honey
- 3 tbsp water

### For Serving

- 1 cup dry white rice (1/3 cup dry per serving)
- Sesame seeds
- Reserved green onion tops

**Prep Notes:**

- Press tofu at least 20 minutes before cooking: wrap the block in a clean towel and set something heavy on top. The drier the tofu, the better it crisps — don't skip this.
- Use the remaining half bell pepper from the curry stuffed cabbage cups if available; supplement with a new one as needed.
- Use 2–3 cups from the remaining 0.75 head of cabbage from this week.
- Whisk the sauce together before starting — it goes in fast once the vegetables are ready.
- Cook the tofu in a single layer and resist stirring; it needs contact time to develop a crust.
- Stir-fry vegetables in order of density: hard veg first, tender veg last.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Cook the rice.** Start 1 cup white rice in a saucepan with 2 cups water and a pinch of salt. Bring to a boil, reduce to a low simmer, cover, and cook 15–18 minutes. Remove from heat and let steam, covered, 5 minutes.

2. **Whisk the sauce.** Combine gochujang, soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, honey, and water. Whisk until smooth. Set aside.

3. **Sear the tofu.** Heat 0.5 tbsp avocado oil in a large skillet or wok over high heat until shimmering. Add tofu in a single layer — work in batches if needed. Cook undisturbed 3–4 minutes until golden and crisp on the bottom. Flip and cook another 2–3 minutes. Transfer to a plate.

4. **Stir-fry the vegetables.** Heat remaining 0.5 tbsp avocado oil in the same pan over high heat. Add carrots and cook 2 minutes. Add mushrooms and cabbage and cook 2–3 minutes until softened and taking on a little color. Add garlic, ginger, and green onion whites to the center of the pan and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant, then stir into the vegetables. Add bell pepper and snap peas and cook 1–2 minutes — keep them crisp.

5. **Combine and sauce.** Return tofu to the pan. Pour sauce over everything and toss to coat. Cook 1–2 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly and glazes everything. Taste — add a splash more soy sauce for salt or more gochujang for heat.

6. **Serve.** Divide over steamed rice into 3 portions. Garnish with sesame seeds and green onion tops.

7. **Store.** Refrigerate remaining portions in an airtight container up to 4 days. Reheat in a skillet over medium with a splash of water to loosen the sauce, or microwave 2 minutes.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~420 per serving with rice (tofu ~110, sauce ~75, vegetables ~55, rice ~165, oil ~15); ~255 cal without rice.
- **Gochujang heat**: varies by brand — Mae Ploy and CJ run hotter. Start with 2 tbsp in the sauce if heat-sensitive and taste before adding the rest.
- **Tofu crisping**: get solid color on at least 2 sides and move on — it doesn't need to be perfect all around.
- **Cabbage**: uses 2–3 cups from the remaining head this week, which also appeared in the curry stuffed cabbage cups.
