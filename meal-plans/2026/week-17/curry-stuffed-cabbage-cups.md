# Curry Stuffed Cabbage Cups with Chicken, Rice & Bell Pepper

Quarter of a green cabbage separated into 2 natural leaf cups, pre-roasted in the air fryer for caramelized edges, then filled with boldly spiced chicken, white rice, bell pepper, and diced tomatoes in a light broth base — finished in the air fryer with a crispy wheat germ topping. ~510 calories.

## Ingredients

### Cabbage

- 0.25 head green cabbage, quartered through the core and gently separated into 2 cup-shaped layers

### Filling

- 1 boneless, skinless chicken breast (~6 oz), cut into 0.5-inch cubes
- 0.25 cup dry white rice
- 1 tsp avocado oil
- 0.5 medium bell pepper, finely diced (any color)
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 0.5 tsp fresh ginger, grated
- 1.5 tsp curry spice blend, plus more to taste
- 0.25 cup canned diced tomatoes, drained
- 3 tbsp low-sodium chicken broth
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- Squeeze of fresh lemon juice

### Topping

- 2 tbsp wheat germ
- Pinch of curry spice blend
- Avocado oil spray

### Garnish

- 3 tbsp plain Greek yogurt
- 2 tbsp fresh cilantro, roughly chopped
- Lemon wedge

**Prep Notes:**

- To form the cups: hold the cabbage quarter core-side down and gently press the outer leaf away from the inner layers until you have 2 distinct cup shapes. Leave the innermost leaves slightly nested together for a thicker base.
- The tomatoes and broth replace coconut milk as the binder; drain the tomatoes well so the filling stays moist rather than wet.
- Taste the filling before stuffing and add more curry spice if needed — it should be assertively seasoned.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Pre-roast the cabbage cups.** Brush or spray the 2 cabbage cups lightly with avocado oil and season with a pinch of salt. Air fry at 400°F for 10–12 minutes until edges begin to char and leaves soften. Remove and set aside on the tray or a plate.

2. **Cook the rice.** Combine rice with 0.5 cup water and a pinch of salt in a small saucepan. Bring to a boil, reduce to a low simmer, cover, and cook 15–18 minutes until water is absorbed. Remove from heat and let steam, covered, 5 minutes. Fluff with a fork.

3. **Cook the aromatics and bell pepper.** While the rice cooks, heat 1 tsp avocado oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add diced bell pepper and cook 3–4 minutes until softened. Add garlic and ginger and cook 1–2 minutes until fragrant.

4. **Cook the chicken.** Add the chicken cubes in a single layer. Season with the curry spice blend, salt, and black pepper. Cook 3–4 minutes without stirring to get some browning, then stir and cook another 2–3 minutes until cooked through.

5. **Build the filling.** Stir in drained diced tomatoes and broth. Cook 2 minutes, scraping up any browned bits. Fold in the cooked rice. Squeeze in a little lemon juice, taste, and adjust salt and curry spice. The filling should be moist but not loose.

6. **Stuff the cups.** Spoon the filling evenly between the 2 pre-roasted cabbage cups, pressing gently to pack. Mound slightly above the rim.

7. **Add topping.** Stir wheat germ with a pinch of curry spice. Sprinkle evenly over each stuffed cup. Spray lightly with avocado oil.

8. **Finish in the air fryer.** Air fry at 370°F for 8–10 minutes until topping is golden and crisp and cabbage edges are fully tender.

9. **Garnish and serve.** Dollop Greek yogurt over each cup. Scatter fresh cilantro on top. Serve with a lemon wedge squeezed over just before eating.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~510 total (chicken ~185, rice ~160, cabbage ~25, bell pepper ~15, tomatoes ~10, avocado oil ~55, wheat germ ~30, Greek yogurt ~30).
- **Curry spice blend**: Use 1.5 tsp as a baseline — assertive seasoning is needed to carry the dish without coconut milk or a fat-rich sauce. Taste after building the filling and add up to 0.5 tsp more if needed.
- **Cabbage**: 0.75 head of cabbage remains — plan other meals to use it up (braised cabbage side, slaw, or added to soup).
