# Pork Carrot Miracle Noodle Soup

Tender shredded or sliced pork simmered in a ginger-forward broth with sweet carrots, aromatic vegetables, and miracle noodles. High volume, deeply warming, and satisfying without the calorie cost of real noodles. ~290 calories.

## Ingredients

### Broth Base

- 2.5 cups water
- 1.5 tsp salt-free chicken or pork bouillon powder
- 1 tsp soy sauce (low-sodium)
- 1 tsp fish sauce (adds depth; substitute extra soy sauce if preferred)
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 0.5 tsp onion powder
- 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated (or 0.5 tsp ground)
- 0.25 tsp white pepper
- 0.25 tsp five spice powder (subtle, adds warmth and complexity)
- 1 star anise (optional, remove before serving)

### Pork

- 1 boneless pork chop or tenderloin piece (5-6 oz), ~160 cal
- 1 tsp avocado oil
- Salt and white pepper

### Vegetables

- 2 medium carrots, peeled and sliced into thin rounds, ~50 cal
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 0.5 tsp avocado oil (for sautéing)
- 2 stalks celery, sliced (optional, adds volume)
- 2 green onions, sliced (whites for cooking, greens for garnish)

### Noodles

- 1 package (7-8 oz) miracle noodles (shirataki), rinsed and drained well, ~15 cal

### Finishing

- 1 tbsp white miso paste, ~35 cal
- 1 tsp sesame oil (drizzle at the end)
- 1 tsp rice vinegar or lime juice (brightens the broth)
- Red pepper flakes or chili oil to taste
- Fresh cilantro or extra green onion tops

**Prep Notes:**

- Sear the pork in a separate step before adding to the soup — browning the outside creates fond and adds color and depth to the broth. Don't skip this.
- The pork can either be simmered whole in the broth and shredded (more tender, takes longer), or seared and sliced thin for a quicker approach. Either works.
- Miracle noodles must be thoroughly rinsed under cold water for 30+ seconds — the packing liquid has a strong odor that fully disappears with rinsing.
- Dry-toast miracle noodles in a dry skillet for 2-3 minutes after rinsing — removes residual moisture and gives a chewier, more pasta-like texture.
- Add the miracle noodles at the very end, just to warm through — they're already cooked and don't need more time in the broth.
- Whisk miso into a small amount of hot broth in a separate bowl before stirring it into the pot — this prevents lumps. Never boil miso; add it off the heat or at a bare simmer to preserve its flavor and probiotic cultures.
- Five spice is subtle at 0.25 tsp — it adds a back-of-the-palate warmth without the dish tasting overtly Chinese five spice.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Sear Pork**:
   - Season pork with salt and white pepper. Heat avocado oil in a medium saucepan over medium-high heat.
   - Sear pork 2-3 minutes per side until browned. Remove and set aside. (It will finish cooking in the broth.)

2. **Sauté Aromatics**:
   - In the same pot over medium heat, add 0.5 tsp avocado oil. Sauté garlic, ginger, and green onion whites for 1 minute until fragrant.

3. **Build the Broth**:
   - Add water, bouillon, soy sauce, fish sauce, garlic powder, onion powder, white pepper, five spice, and star anise if using. Stir to combine.
   - Add carrots and celery. Return seared pork to the pot (whole or cut into thick slices).
   - Bring to a gentle simmer. Cook 15-20 minutes until carrots are tender and pork is fully cooked through (145°F). Remove pork and slice or shred.

4. **Prep Miracle Noodles**:
   - While soup simmers, rinse miracle noodles thoroughly under cold water for 30 seconds.
   - In a dry skillet over medium heat, cook noodles 2-3 minutes until mostly dry and slightly chewy. Set aside.

5. **Finish**:
   - Remove star anise. Return sliced or shredded pork to the pot.
   - Add miracle noodles. Stir in sesame oil and rice vinegar.
   - Remove from heat or reduce to a bare simmer. Ladle a small amount of broth into a bowl, whisk in the miso paste until smooth, then pour back into the pot. Stir to combine. Do not boil after adding miso.
   - Taste and adjust seasoning.

6. **Serve**:
   - Ladle into a large bowl. Top with green onion tops, fresh cilantro, and chili oil or red pepper flakes.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~325 total (pork ~160 + carrots ~50 + miracle noodles ~15 + miso ~35 + broth/aromatics/oil ~65).
- Five spice and star anise give this soup a slightly more complex, Asian-style broth character compared to a Western-style pork soup — deeply aromatic without being heavy.
- Fish sauce is optional but adds significant umami depth at minimal calories; it's barely detectable as "fishy" in a broth — it reads as savory and complex.
