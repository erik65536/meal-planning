# Pan Pork Chop with Brussels Sprouts and Cremini Mushrooms

Bone-in or boneless pork chop seared in a cast iron skillet, rested and finished with a quick pan sauce, served alongside caramelized brussels sprouts and umami-rich cremini mushrooms with garlic and thyme. The mushrooms add deep savory depth and make the whole plate more satisfying. ~430 calories.

## Ingredients

### Pork Chop

- 1 boneless pork chop (6-7 oz), ~200 cal
- 0.5 tsp avocado oil
- 0.5 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.5 tsp garlic powder
- 0.5 tsp onion powder
- 0.25 tsp dried rosemary, crumbled
- 0.25 tsp black pepper
- Salt to taste

### Quick Pan Sauce

- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 0.25 cup low-sodium chicken broth
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard
- 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves (or 0.5 tsp dried)
- 1 tsp lemon juice
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Brussels Sprouts and Mushrooms

- 8 oz brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved, ~80 cal
- 4 oz cremini mushrooms, quartered, ~25 cal
- 1.5 tsp olive oil
- 3 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
- 0.5 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.5 tsp dried thyme
- 0.25 tsp red pepper flakes
- Salt and black pepper to taste

**Prep Notes:**

- Bring the pork chop to room temperature for 15-20 minutes before cooking — a cold chop goes into thermal shock in a hot pan and cooks unevenly, leaving a grey band around the edges.
- Pat the pork chop completely dry before seasoning — a wet surface steams instead of sears, and you want a deep brown crust.
- Cast iron is ideal here; a stainless steel skillet also works well. Non-stick won't develop the same crust or the fond needed for the pan sauce.
- Mushrooms need space to brown — don't crowd them. Cook in a hot, dry pan (or with minimal oil) and don't stir for the first 2-3 minutes. Mushrooms that are crowded or stirred too early steam and turn grey instead of caramelizing.
- The pan sauce uses the pork fond — don't wipe the pan after the chop. Deglaze while the pan is still hot to lift all that flavor.
- Pork is safe at 145°F with a 3-minute rest. A 0.75-inch chop takes about 4-5 minutes per side over medium-high heat.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Prep**:
   - Let pork chop sit at room temperature 15-20 minutes.
   - Mix smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, rosemary, and pepper. Pat chop dry and rub all over with the spice mix plus salt.

2. **Roast Brussels Sprouts**:
   - Toss halved brussels sprouts with 1 tsp olive oil, garlic slices, smoked paprika, thyme, red pepper flakes, salt, and pepper.
   - Roast at 400°F cut-side down for 20-22 minutes until deeply caramelized. (Can also cook in a second skillet on the stovetop while the pork sears.)

3. **Sear Pork Chop**:
   - Heat a cast iron or heavy skillet over medium-high heat until very hot. Add avocado oil.
   - Place pork chop in the pan. Sear undisturbed 4-5 minutes until a deep brown crust forms. Flip and cook 4-5 minutes more until internal temp reaches 140-145°F.
   - Remove chop and rest on a plate for 5 minutes (temp will carry up to 145°F while resting).

4. **Cook Mushrooms**:
   - In the same skillet over medium-high heat, add remaining 0.5 tsp olive oil. Add mushrooms in a single layer.
   - Cook undisturbed 3 minutes until browned on the bottom. Stir and cook another 2-3 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.

5. **Make Pan Sauce**:
   - Add garlic to the mushrooms and cook 30 seconds. Deglaze with chicken broth, scraping up the brown fond.
   - Stir in Dijon mustard, thyme, and lemon juice. Simmer 1-2 minutes until slightly reduced.

6. **Plate**:
   - Slice pork chop if desired. Plate alongside brussels sprouts and mushrooms.
   - Spoon pan sauce over the pork and mushrooms.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~430 total (pork ~200 + brussels sprouts ~80 + mushrooms ~25 + olive/avocado oil ~90 + broth/sauce ~35).
- Cremini mushrooms have significantly more umami than white button mushrooms — worth seeking out. Shiitake mushrooms are an even bolder substitution.
- The Dijon pan sauce is tangy, herby, and rich without any butter or cream — it brightens the whole plate.
