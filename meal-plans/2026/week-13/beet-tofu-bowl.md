# Beet and Silken Tofu Bowl

Silken tofu blended with roasted beets into a vibrant, creamy, magenta-purple base — earthy, slightly sweet, and velvety smooth. Topped with caramelized cauliflower and broccoli and golden yellow pepper for striking color contrast. Fully vegetarian and nutrient-dense. ~390 calories.

## Ingredients

### Beet Tofu Base

- 3 medium beets (about 15 oz raw), roasted and peeled, ~105 cal
- 6 oz silken tofu, ~60 cal
- 1 tbsp lemon juice
- 1 clove garlic
- 0.5 tsp cumin
- 0.5 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.25 tsp ground coriander
- 1-3 tbsp water (to adjust consistency)

### Roasted Vegetables

- 1 cup cauliflower florets, cut into small pieces, ~25 cal
- 1 cup broccoli florets, cut into small pieces, ~30 cal
- 0.5 yellow bell pepper, sliced into strips, ~20 cal
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp za'atar (or Italian seasoning)
- 0.5 tsp garlic powder
- 0.5 tsp smoked paprika

### Toppings

- 0.5 oz blue cheese, crumbled, ~50 cal
- 2 tbsp plain Greek yogurt (dolloped on top), ~20 cal
- 1 tbsp fresh dill or mint, roughly chopped
- 0.5 tsp za'atar for finishing
- 0.5 tsp mixed white and black sesame seeds
- Squeeze of lemon juice
**Prep Notes:**

- Roast the beets whole and unpeeled — wrap each beet in foil and roast at 400°F for 45-55 minutes until a knife slides through with no resistance. The skins slip off easily once cooled. This can be done a day ahead.
- The silken tofu does not need to be pressed — its soft, custard-like texture is exactly what creates the creamy base when blended. Firm or extra-firm tofu won't work here.
- Blend the base until completely smooth — at least 60-90 seconds in a blender or with an immersion blender. Any remaining texture from the beets will be grainy.
- The base should be thick and spreadable, like hummus — add water 1 tbsp at a time to reach the right consistency. Too thin and the toppings sink.
- Cut cauliflower and broccoli into similarly sized small florets so they roast evenly — too large and the centers stay raw while the edges char.
- Spread the beet-tofu base in a thick layer across a wide bowl or plate, then pile the roasted vegetables on top — this is a composed bowl, not a mixed bowl. The color contrast of magenta base + green broccoli + white cauliflower + yellow pepper is the visual payoff.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Roast Beets** (can do ahead):
   - Preheat oven to 400°F. Wrap beets individually in foil.
   - Roast 45-55 minutes until tender. Cool 10 minutes, then slip skins off under running water.
   - Roughly chop beets and set aside.

2. **Roast Vegetables**:
   - Increase oven to 425°F (or roast beets at this temp if timing works).
   - Toss cauliflower, broccoli, and yellow bell pepper strips with olive oil, za'atar, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
   - Spread on a baking sheet in a single layer — don't crowd or they'll steam.
   - Roast 22-25 minutes, flipping once halfway, until cauliflower is golden and broccoli has crispy dark edges. Set aside.

3. **Blend Beet-Tofu Base**:
   - Add roasted beet pieces, silken tofu, lemon juice, garlic, cumin, smoked paprika, coriander, salt, and pepper to a blender.
   - Blend until completely smooth, at least 60-90 seconds. Add water 1 tbsp at a time to reach a thick, spreadable consistency.
   - Taste and adjust lemon, salt, and spices.

4. **Assemble**:
   - Spread beet-tofu base in a thick layer across a wide bowl or plate.
   - Pile roasted vegetables in the center.
   - Add dollops of Greek yogurt, crumble blue cheese over the top, and scatter fresh dill or mint.
   - Squeeze lemon juice over everything, dust with za'atar, and sprinkle mixed white and black sesame seeds.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~335 total (beets ~105 + tofu ~60 + cauliflower ~25 + broccoli ~30 + bell pepper ~20 + olive oil ~60 + blue cheese ~50 + yogurt ~20 + sesame seeds ~15).
- The color palette is the point: deep magenta base, bright green broccoli, white cauliflower, pale yellow pepper, white yogurt/blue cheese, and black and white sesame specks. Photograph from directly above.
- Blue cheese adds a pungent, creamy tang that cuts through the sweetness of the beets far more aggressively than feta — a small amount goes a long way.
- Za'atar on the roasted vegetables and as a finish gives an herby, tangy, sesame note that plays well with both the beets and the yogurt.
