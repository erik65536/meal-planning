# Pork Black Bean Sauce Stir Fry

Cubed pork seared hot and tossed with frozen vegetables in a deeply savory Chinese black bean sauce — fermented, funky, garlicky, and a little spicy. A completely different flavor profile from the gochujang or teriyaki versions. Served over the vegetables as-is. ~450 calories.

## Ingredients

### Pork

- 1 boneless pork chop (6 oz), cut into 0.75-inch cubes, ~180 cal
- 1 tsp avocado oil
- 0.5 tsp white pepper
- 0.5 tsp garlic powder
- 0.5 tsp Chinese 7 spice

### Black Bean Sauce

- 1.5 tbsp Chinese black bean garlic sauce (Lee Kum Kee or similar)
- 1 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
- 1 tsp Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry, or rice vinegar as substitute)
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp sugar or honey
- 0.5 tsp chili garlic sauce or sambal oelek (adjust to heat preference)
- 2 tbsp water
- 1 tsp cornstarch (stir into the water to make a slurry — thickens the sauce)

### Stir Fry

- 1 bag (12 oz) frozen stir fry vegetables (broccoli, snap peas, carrots, water chestnuts), ~180 cal
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
- 0.5 tsp avocado oil (for vegetables)

### Finishing

- 2 green onions, thinly sliced
- 1 tsp sesame seeds
- Extra chili garlic sauce to taste

**Prep Notes:**

- Chinese black bean garlic sauce is already very salty — taste the sauce before adding soy sauce and reduce if needed. Some brands are saltier than others.
- The cornstarch slurry is key: stir the cornstarch into the 2 tbsp water first, then add this to the rest of the sauce. This gives the sauce a glossy, clingy texture that coats the pork and vegetables properly.
- Mix the sauce in a small bowl before starting — stir fry moves too fast to measure mid-cook.
- Pat pork cubes dry before searing — moisture prevents browning and you want a good crust before adding sauce.
- Shaoxing wine adds an authentic depth that rice vinegar can't fully replicate, but either works.
- Black bean sauce has a strong fermented flavor — 1.5 tbsp is the right amount for one serving. More than 2 tbsp becomes overpowering.
- Chinese 7 spice goes on the pork as a dry rub, not into the sauce — its warm, aromatic notes (star anise, cinnamon, clove, fennel) perfume the crust during searing and add a background layer without muddying the sauce.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Make Sauce**:
   - In a small bowl, whisk together black bean garlic sauce, soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, sesame oil, sugar, chili garlic sauce, water, and cornstarch until smooth. Set aside.

2. **Sear Pork**:
   - Pat pork cubes dry. Season with white pepper, garlic powder, and Chinese 7 spice.
   - Heat avocado oil in a large skillet or wok over high heat until just smoking.
   - Add pork in a single layer. Sear undisturbed 90 seconds, flip, cook another 60-90 seconds until browned and cooked through (145°F). Remove and set aside.

3. **Cook Vegetables**:
   - Add remaining 0.5 tsp avocado oil to the same pan over high heat.
   - Add frozen stir fry vegetables directly from the bag. Spread flat and cook undisturbed 2-3 minutes until charred on one side.
   - Stir and cook another 2 minutes. Add garlic and ginger; stir fry 60 seconds until fragrant.

4. **Combine & Glaze**:
   - Return pork to the pan. Pour sauce over everything. Toss to coat.
   - Cook 60-90 seconds, stirring constantly, until sauce thickens, turns glossy, and clings. The cornstarch will activate and coat everything.

5. **Serve**:
   - Plate and top with green onions, sesame seeds, and extra chili garlic sauce.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~450 total (pork ~180 + vegetables ~180 + black bean sauce ~50 + oil ~40).
- Lee Kum Kee black bean garlic sauce is widely available at most grocery stores with an Asian food section. It comes in a glass jar and keeps refrigerated for months.
- This dish has deep umami from the fermented black beans — it tastes significantly different from soy-based stir fry sauces. More savory, funky, and complex.
