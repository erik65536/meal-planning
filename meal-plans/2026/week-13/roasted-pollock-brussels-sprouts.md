# Roasted Pollock and Brussels Sprouts

Sheet pan white fish dinner — three pollock fillets roasted alongside crispy caramelized brussels sprouts with lemon, garlic, and a smoky herb crust. Light, filling, and ready in under 30 minutes. ~790 calories.

## Ingredients

### Pollock

- 3 pollock fillets (6 oz each), patted dry, ~450 cal
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1.5 tsp smoked paprika
- 1.5 tsp garlic powder
- 0.75 tsp onion powder
- 0.75 tsp dried thyme
- 0.5 tsp black pepper
- 0.25 tsp cayenne
- Salt to taste

### Brussels Sprouts

- 10 oz brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved, ~100 cal
- 1.5 tsp olive oil
- 3 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
- 0.5 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.5 tsp garlic powder
- 0.25 tsp red pepper flakes
- Salt and black pepper to taste

### Finishing

- 1 lemon, halved (one half for juice, one half for serving)
- Zest of 1 lemon (before juicing — adds bright citrus punch in place of capers)
- 1 tbsp fresh parsley, roughly chopped

**Prep Notes:**

- Halve brussels sprouts through the stem so they lay flat cut-side down — this maximizes caramelization and crispiness on the cut face.
- Give brussels sprouts a 10-minute head start before adding the fish — they take longer and need a fully hot pan to caramelize properly.
- Pat the pollock completely dry before seasoning — any surface moisture turns into steam in the oven and softens the crust.
- Pollock is a very lean fish and cooks fast; a 6 oz fillet typically needs only 12-15 minutes at 425°F. Pull it when it flakes easily at the thickest point.
- Don't crowd the sprouts or the fish — with 3 fillets you'll likely need two sheet pans. Put sprouts on one pan, fish on another, or split sprouts across both. Crowding causes steaming instead of roasting.
- Zest the lemon before cutting and juicing it — much easier that way. Zest goes on at the end as a finishing garnish, not during roasting.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Preheat**: Oven to 425°F. Line a large baking sheet with parchment.

2. **Roast Brussels Sprouts**:
   - Toss halved brussels sprouts with olive oil, garlic slices, smoked paprika, garlic powder, red pepper flakes, salt, and pepper.
   - Spread cut-side down on the baking sheet in a single layer. Roast 10 minutes.

3. **Season Fish**:
   - While sprouts roast, mix smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, thyme, pepper, and cayenne.
   - Rub each pollock fillet with olive oil, then press the spice mix evenly over the top and sides.

4. **Add Fish to Pan**:
   - After 10 minutes, flip brussels sprouts. Place pollock fillets on the pan (or a second pan if needed — don't crowd).
   - Roast another 12-15 minutes until fish flakes easily at the thickest point and sprouts are caramelized and crispy.

5. **Finish & Serve**:
   - Squeeze half a lemon over everything. Scatter fresh parsley and lemon zest.
   - Serve with the remaining lemon half on the side.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~790 total (pollock ~450 + brussels sprouts ~100 + olive oil ~120 + aromatics ~20 + lemon ~10).
- Pollock is mild and slightly sweet — it takes seasoning well and is nearly identical in texture and flavor to cod at a lower price point.
- Brussels sprouts are done when the cut faces are deep brown and the outer leaves are charred and crispy. Don't pull them early — the caramelization is the payoff.
