# Mapo Tofu over Rice

Classic Sichuan mapo tofu — silken tofu in a fiery, numbing, deeply savory sauce of doubanjiang, ground pork, fermented black beans, and Sichuan peppercorns. Served over a small amount of white rice to soak up the sauce. Bold, complex, and legitimately one of the most flavorful dishes in Chinese cooking. ~490 calories.

## Ingredients

### Pork

- 2.5 oz ground pork (or finely minced pork chop), ~100 cal

### Tofu

- 7-8 oz silken or soft tofu, cut into 0.75-inch cubes, ~70 cal

### Sauce

- 1.5 tbsp doubanjiang (Sichuan chili bean paste — this is the essential ingredient)
- 1 tbsp fermented black beans (douchi), roughly chopped (or 0.5 tbsp black bean garlic sauce)
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
- 0.75 cup low-sodium chicken broth
- 1 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp sugar
- 1 tsp cornstarch dissolved in 1 tbsp water (slurry)

### Sichuan Spice

- 0.5 tsp Sichuan peppercorns, toasted and ground (essential for the numbing tingle — málà effect)
- 0.25 tsp red pepper flakes or extra doubanjiang to taste
- 0.5 tsp chili oil (finishing)

### Aromatics

- 1 tsp avocado oil
- 0.5 tsp sesame oil (for cooking aromatics)
- 2 green onions, sliced (whites for cooking, greens for garnish)

### Rice

- 0.3 cup dry white rice, cooked (~0.75 cup cooked), ~160 cal

**Prep Notes:**

- Doubanjiang is non-negotiable for authentic mapo tofu — it provides the deep red color, fermented funk, chili heat, and savory backbone. Lee Kum Kee and Pixian brands are widely available online or at Asian grocery stores. No substitution replicates it.
- Silken or soft tofu is correct for this dish — firmer tofu won't absorb the sauce in the same way and loses the custard-soft texture contrast with the spicy sauce. Handle silken tofu gently; it breaks apart easily.
- To prevent tofu from crumbling, briefly simmer the cubes in lightly salted water for 2-3 minutes before adding to the sauce — this firms them up just enough to hold their shape while staying soft inside.
- Toast Sichuan peppercorns in a dry pan 60-90 seconds until fragrant, then grind in a mortar or spice grinder. The freshly ground peppercorns provide the characteristic málà numbing sensation — pre-ground loses potency quickly.
- Doubanjiang is already very salty — taste before adding soy sauce and reduce if needed.
- The cornstarch slurry is added at the end and gives the sauce the right silky, coating consistency. Without it, the sauce stays watery.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Start Rice**:
   - Cook rice according to package directions. Use a small amount — this is meant to be a small serving to soak up sauce.

2. **Prep Tofu**:
   - Cut tofu into 0.75-inch cubes. Bring a small pot of lightly salted water to a gentle simmer. Add tofu cubes and simmer 2-3 minutes. Drain carefully and set aside.

3. **Toast and Grind Sichuan Peppercorns**:
   - In a dry skillet over medium heat, toast peppercorns 60-90 seconds until fragrant. Grind finely. Set aside.

4. **Cook Pork**:
   - Heat avocado oil in a wok or skillet over medium-high heat. Add ground pork and cook, breaking up, until fully browned and starting to crisp, about 3-4 minutes. Push to the side.

5. **Build Sauce Base**:
   - Add sesame oil to the pan. Add doubanjiang and cook, stirring constantly, for 60-90 seconds until the oil turns deep red — this blooms the chili paste and is critical for flavor and color.
   - Add fermented black beans, garlic, ginger, and green onion whites. Stir fry 60 seconds.

6. **Add Broth and Tofu**:
   - Pour in chicken broth, soy sauce, and sugar. Stir to combine with the pork.
   - Gently slide in the pre-simmered tofu cubes. Spoon sauce over the tofu — don't stir aggressively or the cubes will break.
   - Simmer 3-4 minutes, occasionally spooning sauce over the tofu.

7. **Thicken and Finish**:
   - Stir cornstarch slurry, then pour around the edges of the pan (not directly on tofu). Gently swirl the pan to incorporate.
   - Cook 30-60 seconds until sauce thickens and turns glossy.
   - Remove from heat. Drizzle with sesame oil and chili oil.

8. **Plate**:
   - Spoon rice into a bowl. Ladle mapo tofu and sauce over and around the rice.
   - Garnish with ground Sichuan peppercorns, green onion greens, and extra chili oil.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~490 total (pork ~100 + tofu ~70 + rice ~160 + sauce/aromatics/oil ~160).
- The málà (numbing-spicy) sensation from Sichuan peppercorns is unique — not heat exactly, more like a gentle electric tingle on the lips and tongue. It's what makes this dish distinctive from other chili-based dishes.
- Doubanjiang, fermented black beans, and Sichuan peppercorns are all available online (Amazon) or at any Asian grocery store. They last a very long time refrigerated.
- The small amount of rice is intentional — enough to absorb the sauce and add starch without making this a high-carb meal. The tofu is the protein and volume star.
