# Pork Roasted Tomato and Olive Wrap

Cumin-coriander braised shredded pork in three medium tortillas with jammy roasted tomatoes, briny kalamata olives, fresh spinach blend, pickled red onion, blue cheese crumbles, and a smear of Greek yogurt sauce. Mediterranean-adjacent, bold, and satisfying. ~580 calories.

## Ingredients

### Pork

- 1 boneless pork chop (6 oz), braised and shredded, ~180 cal
- 1 tsp avocado oil
- 0.25 cup low-sodium chicken broth (for braising)
- 1 tsp cumin
- 0.5 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.5 tsp coriander
- 0.5 tsp garlic powder
- 0.25 tsp black pepper
- 0.25 tsp cayenne
- Salt to taste

### Roasted Tomatoes

- 6-8 cherry tomatoes, halved
- 0.5 tsp olive oil
- 0.25 tsp dried oregano
- Pinch of salt

### Wrap

- 3 medium tortillas (80 cal each), ~240 cal
- 8 kalamata olives, pitted and sliced, ~40 cal
- 1 cup baby spinach blend
- 2 tbsp pickled red onion (see prep notes)
- 0.5 oz blue cheese, crumbled, ~50 cal

### Yogurt Sauce

- 2 tbsp plain Greek yogurt (0% or 2%), ~20 cal
- 1 tsp lemon juice
- 0.5 tsp dried oregano
- 1 small clove garlic, minced or pressed
- Salt and pepper to taste

**Prep Notes:**

- Roast the tomatoes first — halve them, toss in olive oil and oregano, and roast at 400°F for 15-18 minutes until they collapse and caramelize slightly. The jammy concentrated flavor is worth the extra step.
- For quick pickled red onion: thinly slice a quarter of a red onion, soak in 2 tbsp red wine vinegar + pinch of salt + pinch of sugar for at least 20 minutes while the pork cooks. They'll turn bright pink and soften.
- For shredded pork: sear the chop first for color and fond, then add a splash of broth, cover, and braise on low heat for 20-25 minutes until the meat pulls apart easily with two forks. The braising liquid can be spooned over the shredded pork for extra flavor.
- Warm each tortilla briefly in a dry skillet or directly over a gas burner for 15-20 seconds per side — a warm, pliable tortilla wraps much better and adds a slight char flavor.
- Spread the yogurt sauce on the tortilla first as a binder; this keeps all the toppings from sliding out.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Roast Tomatoes**:
   - Preheat oven to 400°F. Halve cherry tomatoes, toss with olive oil, oregano, and salt.
   - Spread cut-side up on a small baking sheet or oven-safe pan. Roast 15-18 minutes until jammy and slightly caramelized. Set aside.

2. **Make Yogurt Sauce**:
   - Mix Greek yogurt, lemon juice, oregano, and garlic in a small bowl. Season with salt and pepper. Set aside.

3. **Quick Pickle the Onion**:
   - Slice red onion thinly. Combine with red wine vinegar, salt, and sugar. Let sit at least 20 minutes.

4. **Braise and Shred Pork**:
   - Season pork chop all over with cumin, smoked paprika, coriander, garlic powder, cayenne, and salt.
   - Heat avocado oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Sear pork 2-3 minutes per side until browned.
   - Reduce heat to low. Add chicken broth, cover, and braise 20-25 minutes until the pork is tender and pulls apart easily.
   - Remove pork and shred with two forks. Spoon any remaining braising liquid over the shredded pork.

5. **Assemble**:
   - Warm each tortilla in a dry skillet 15-20 seconds per side.
   - Spread yogurt sauce across the center of each. Layer with spinach blend, roasted tomatoes, shredded pork, olives, pickled onion, and blue cheese crumbles.
   - Fold sides in, then roll tightly from the bottom. Repeat for all three wraps.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~580 total (pork ~180 + tortillas ~240 + blue cheese ~50 + olives ~40 + yogurt sauce ~20 + tomatoes and toppings ~50).
- Kalamata olives are salty — taste before adding extra salt to the pork seasoning.
- Pickled onion keeps in the fridge for 1-2 weeks; make a bigger batch and use on other meals this week.
