# Breaded Salmon with Roasted Potatoes and Tomatoes, Homemade Tzatziki

Crispy pre-seasoned breaded salmon over garlicky roasted potatoes and burst campari tomatoes, finished with a fresh tzatziki made from the cucumber centers left over from the cucumber boats. ~540 calories.

## Ingredients

### Salmon

- 1 pre-seasoned and breaded salmon fillet (from frozen or fresh), ~285 cal

### Roasted Potatoes & Tomatoes

- 6 oz small yellow or baby potatoes, halved
- 1 cup campari tomatoes, halved
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 3 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
- 1 tsp Italian seasoning
- 0.5 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.5 tsp garlic powder
- 0.25 tsp red pepper flakes
- Salt and black pepper to taste

### Tzatziki

- Reserved cucumber centers from [cucumber-chicken-boats-roasted-asparagus.md](cucumber-chicken-boats-roasted-asparagus.md), grated and squeezed dry (~0.5 cup grated)
- 0.5 cup plain nonfat Greek yogurt
- 1-2 cloves garlic, finely grated or minced
- 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 1 tbsp fresh dill, finely chopped (or 1 tsp dried)
- 0.5 tsp olive oil
- 0.25 tsp white or black pepper
- Salt to taste

**Prep Notes:**

- Make the tzatziki first — it needs at least 20 minutes in the fridge for the garlic to mellow and the flavors to come together. An hour is even better; make it right when you get home.
- To prep the cucumber for tzatziki: grate the reserved centers on a box grater, then pile the grated cucumber onto a clean kitchen towel or paper towels and squeeze firmly. Remove as much water as possible — this is what keeps tzatziki thick instead of soupy.
- Halve the potatoes so the cut side sits flat against the hot pan — this is what creates the golden crust rather than just steaming.
- Campari tomatoes are cut-side down on the pan — the cut surface caramelizes and concentrates their sweetness during roasting. They'll soften and jam up around the potatoes.
- Check the salmon packaging for specific temperature instructions — follow the package if it differs from 400°F.
- Give potatoes a 10-minute head start before adding salmon so everything finishes together.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Make Tzatziki** (do this first):
   - Grate the reserved cucumber centers on a box grater. Wrap in a towel and squeeze out as much water as possible.
   - In a bowl, stir together squeezed cucumber, Greek yogurt, grated garlic, lemon juice, dill, olive oil, pepper, and salt.
   - Refrigerate for at least 20 minutes before serving.

2. **Preheat**: Oven to 400°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment.

3. **Season Potatoes**:
   - Toss halved potatoes with olive oil, sliced garlic, Italian seasoning, smoked paprika, garlic powder, red pepper flakes, salt, and pepper.
   - Spread cut-side down on the baking sheet. Roast 10 minutes.

4. **Add Tomatoes & Salmon**:
   - After 10 minutes, nestle the halved campari tomatoes cut-side down around the potatoes.
   - Place the breaded salmon fillet on the same pan or a second sheet if needed.
   - Roast another 18-22 minutes until potatoes are golden and tender, tomatoes have softened and caramelized, and salmon is cooked through with crispy breading.

5. **Serve**:
   - Plate salmon alongside the roasted potatoes and tomatoes.
   - Spoon tzatziki generously over the salmon and serve the rest on the side.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~540 total (salmon ~285 + potatoes ~150 + tomatoes ~25 + oil ~60 + tzatziki ~25).
- The tzatziki also works well as a dipping sauce for the roasted broccoli if you have any leftover from the cucumber boats night.
- Any leftover tzatziki keeps refrigerated for 3-4 days — great on wraps or as a vegetable dip.
