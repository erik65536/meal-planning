# Cucumber Chicken Boats with Roasted Broccoli

Hollowed English cucumbers filled with lemony seasoned chicken, roasted red peppers, and peppery greens — fresh, crunchy, and low-calorie. The scooped cucumber centers are reserved for the week's tzatziki. Roasted broccoli on the side. ~355 calories.

## Ingredients

### Cucumber Boats

- 2 English cucumbers, halved lengthwise, centers scooped out and reserved (see Tzatziki in [breaded-salmon-roasted-potatoes-tomatoes-tzatziki.md](breaded-salmon-roasted-potatoes-tomatoes-tzatziki.md))
- 6 oz chicken breast, cooked and diced into 0.5-inch cubes
- 0.5 cup jarred roasted red peppers, drained and sliced into strips
- 2 cups arugula or baby spinach, lightly packed
- 2 tbsp capers, drained
- 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 1 tsp olive oil
- Salt and black pepper to taste

### Chicken Seasoning

- 0.75 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.5 tsp garlic powder
- 0.5 tsp dried oregano
- 0.5 tsp lemon pepper
- 0.25 tsp cumin
- 0.25 tsp onion powder
- Salt to taste

### Lemon-Herb Yogurt Drizzle

- 3 tbsp plain nonfat Greek yogurt
- 1 tsp lemon juice
- 0.5 tsp dried dill (or 1 tsp fresh)
- 1 small clove garlic, finely grated
- Salt to taste

### Roasted Broccoli

- 1 head broccoli (~12 oz), cut into small florets
- 1 tsp olive oil
- 0.5 tsp garlic powder
- 0.25 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.25 tsp onion powder
- Zest of 0.5 lemon
- Salt and black pepper to taste

**Prep Notes:**

- Use a spoon to scoop the seedy center from each cucumber half — you want a clean channel about 0.5 inch deep. Reserve all of the scooped flesh; it goes directly into tzatziki later this week.
- The chicken can be poached, baked, or pan-seared — whatever method you prefer. Season it while it's still hot so the spices bloom and adhere. Dice after it rests.
- Toss the arugula with just the lemon juice and olive oil before loading into the boats — it wilts slightly under the warm chicken, which is fine.
- Mix the yogurt drizzle first and refrigerate while you prep everything else so the garlic mellows.
- A vegetable peeler can score thin stripes down the cucumber skin before halving — purely aesthetic but adds texture and presentation.
- Cut broccoli into small, uniform florets so they roast evenly and the edges get properly crispy. Larger florets stay soft in the middle.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Roast Broccoli**:
   - Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment.
   - Toss broccoli florets with olive oil, garlic powder, smoked paprika, onion powder, lemon zest, salt, and pepper.
   - Spread in a single layer — don't crowd the pan or they'll steam. Roast 18-20 minutes until the edges are charred and florets are crispy. Don't toss partway through; let them sit undisturbed to maximize browning on the bottom.

2. **Cook Chicken**:
   - Season chicken breast on both sides with smoked paprika, garlic powder, oregano, lemon pepper, cumin, onion powder, and salt.
   - Heat a skillet over medium-high heat with a light spray of cooking oil or a small drizzle of avocado oil.
   - Cook 5-6 minutes per side until internal temperature reaches 165°F. Rest 5 minutes, then dice into 0.5-inch cubes.

3. **Make Yogurt Drizzle**:
   - Stir together Greek yogurt, lemon juice, dill, garlic, and salt. Refrigerate until serving.

4. **Prep Cucumbers**:
   - Halve cucumbers lengthwise. Scoop out centers with a spoon; transfer scooped flesh to a container and refrigerate (use this week for tzatziki).
   - Season the cucumber channels lightly with salt and a tiny squeeze of lemon.

5. **Toss Greens**:
   - Toss arugula with lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper.

6. **Assemble**:
   - Layer arugula into the cucumber channels first.
   - Top with diced chicken, then roasted red pepper strips and capers.
   - Drizzle yogurt sauce over the top.

7. **Serve** with roasted broccoli on the side.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~355 total (chicken ~185 + cucumber ~30 + roasted red peppers ~20 + arugula ~10 + broccoli ~60 + yogurt drizzle ~25 + oil ~25).
- This is a lighter meal — works well earlier in the week when you want something fresh and low-effort.
- The chicken can be cooked in a larger batch to use for the [chipotle chicken lettuce tacos](chipotle-chicken-lettuce-tacos.md) later this week.
- Butter lettuce leaves make a good substitute if you want a scoopable rather than boat-style presentation.
