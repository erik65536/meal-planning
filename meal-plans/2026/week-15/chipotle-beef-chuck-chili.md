# Chipotle Beef Chuck Chili

A deep, smoky chili built with tender slow-braised beef chuck, a full can of mixed beans, fire-roasted tomatoes, and serious chipotle heat. A diced potato and mushrooms add bulk and body. Makes 2 generous servings — cook once, eat twice. ~470 calories per serving.

## Ingredients

### Beef

- 8 oz beef chuck, cut into 0.75-inch cubes
- 0.5 tsp salt
- 0.25 tsp black pepper
- 0.5 tbsp avocado oil (for searing)

### Aromatics & Vegetables

- 1 medium yellow onion, diced
- 1 medium bell pepper (any color), diced
- 8 oz mushrooms, sliced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 medium Yukon Gold potato, diced into 0.5-inch pieces (skin on)

### Beans & Tomatoes

- 1 can (15 oz) mixed beans (or pinto/kidney/black), drained and rinsed
- 1 can (14.5 oz) fire-roasted diced tomatoes
- 1 can (14.5 oz) crushed tomatoes
- 2 tbsp tomato paste

### Chipotle & Liquid

- Half a can (~3-4 chipotles in adobo), minced
- All the adobo sauce from the half can
- 1 cup low-sodium beef broth

### Seasoning

- 1.5 tsp cumin
- 1.25 tsp chili powder
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.5 tsp dried oregano
- 0.5 tsp coriander
- 0.25 tsp cayenne
- Salt and black pepper to taste

### Finishing

- 1 tbsp lime juice, stirred in at the end
- Thinly sliced jalapeño or radishes

**Prep Notes:**

- Cut the beef chuck into uniform cubes — they'll break down and become tender during the long braise.
- Searing the beef in batches before braising builds significant flavor. Don't rush this step and don't crowd the pan; if the beef steams instead of sears, you lose the browning.
- The potato absorbs the braising liquid and seasons all the way through — cut it no smaller than 0.5 inch so it holds together rather than dissolving.
- Add mushrooms with the aromatics so they cook down fully and contribute deep umami to the base. They'll shrink dramatically — that's expected.
- This chili thickens as it sits — if it seems loose at the end of cooking, remove the lid and simmer uncovered for 10 minutes.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Sear Beef**:
   - Pat beef cubes dry. Season with salt and pepper.
   - Heat avocado oil in a large Dutch oven or heavy pot over high heat.
   - Sear beef in a single layer, undisturbed, for 2-3 minutes until deeply browned on the bottom. Flip and sear 1-2 more minutes. Work in batches if needed. Transfer seared beef to a plate.

2. **Cook Aromatics**:
   - Reduce heat to medium. In the same pot, add onion, bell pepper, and mushrooms. Cook 5-6 minutes until softened and mushrooms have released their liquid, scraping up any browned bits from the beef.
   - Add garlic, chipotles, and all the adobo sauce. Cook 1 minute until fragrant.
   - Add tomato paste. Stir and cook 2 minutes until the paste darkens slightly and sticks to the bottom.

3. **Build the Chili**:
   - Add cumin, chili powder, smoked paprika, oregano, coriander, and cayenne. Stir into the aromatics for 30 seconds.
   - Return the seared beef to the pot.
   - Pour in fire-roasted tomatoes, crushed tomatoes, and beef broth. Stir to combine, scraping up any remaining browned bits.
   - Add the diced potato.

4. **Simmer**:
   - Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer 60-75 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the beef is fork-tender.

5. **Add Beans**:
   - Add the drained mixed beans. Stir to combine.
   - Simmer uncovered 15-20 minutes until the chili has thickened.

6. **Finish**:
   - Stir in lime juice. Taste and adjust salt and cayenne.
   - If the chili is too thick, add a splash of broth. If too thin, simmer uncovered a few more minutes.

7. **Serve** (both nights):
   - Ladle into bowls. Top with sliced jalapeño or radishes.
   - Night 2: reheat over medium-low with a splash of broth if needed — the chili thickens significantly overnight and tastes even better the second day.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~470 per serving (beef ~210 + beans ~110 + vegetables ~70 + tomatoes ~60 + potato ~60 + broth and oil ~30) — estimate varies with beef fat content.
- Beef chuck is ideal here — the collagen in the connective tissue breaks down into gelatin over the long simmer, making the braising liquid silky and the meat fall-apart tender.
- The other half of the chipotle can is used in the [chipotle chicken lettuce tacos](chipotle-chicken-lettuce-tacos.md).
