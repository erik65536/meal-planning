# Chipotle Chicken Lettuce Tacos

Tender shredded chipotle chicken served in crisp butter lettuce cups with seasoned riced cauliflower, diced tomatoes, and bright garnishes. Low-carb, high-protein, and boldly spiced. ~390 calories.

## Ingredients

### Chipotle Chicken

- 8 oz chicken breast or boneless thighs
- Half a can (~3-4 chipotles in adobo), minced
- All the adobo sauce from the half can
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp cumin
- 0.5 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.5 tsp chili powder
- 0.5 tsp dried oregano
- 0.25 tsp black pepper
- 0.5 tbsp lime juice
- 0.5 tsp avocado oil
- Salt to taste

### Cauliflower Rice

- 1 small head cauliflower, cut into florets
- 0.5 tsp avocado oil
- 0.5 tsp cumin
- 0.25 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp lime juice
- Salt to taste

### Tacos

- 8-10 butter lettuce leaves (Boston bibb), separated into individual cups
- 1 cup cherry tomatoes or 2 Roma tomatoes, diced
- 0.25 red onion, finely diced
- 1 jalapeño, thinly sliced
- Fresh cilantro, roughly chopped
- Lime wedges for serving
- 3-4 radishes, thinly sliced

**Prep Notes:**

- The chicken can be batch-cooked the same day as the cucumber boats — cook a larger breast and shred half for each meal to save time. Store the shredded chipotle chicken refrigerated and reheat quickly before serving.
- To shred the chicken: simmer or bake until fully cooked, then rest 5 minutes. Use two forks, or place the breast in a stand mixer bowl and run on low for 20 seconds — it shreds instantly.
- To rice the cauliflower: cut into florets and pulse in a food processor in batches until broken down into rice-sized pieces. Alternatively, use the large holes on a box grater. Don't over-process or it turns to mush — you want distinct grains.
- The cauliflower rice releases a lot of moisture when it hits the hot pan. Cook it over medium-high heat and don't stir too often — let it sit and evaporate rather than steaming and going soggy.
- Butter lettuce leaves make the ideal cup — peel them carefully to keep the natural bowl shape intact. Romaine hearts work too and hold more filling.
- Dice the tomatoes and red onion, then toss with a squeeze of lime and a pinch of salt to make a quick pico while the chicken cooks.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Marinate Chicken** (20-30 minutes, or overnight):
   - Combine minced chipotles, all the adobo sauce, garlic, cumin, smoked paprika, chili powder, oregano, black pepper, lime juice, avocado oil, and salt in a small bowl.
   - Coat chicken breast or thighs thoroughly. Marinate at least 20 minutes.

2. **Cook Chicken** (choose one method):
   - **Simmer**: Place chicken in a small saucepan with 0.5 cup water and the marinade. Bring to a simmer over medium heat. Cover and cook 15-18 minutes until cooked through. Shred with forks in the pan and let the liquid reduce slightly.
   - **Bake**: Place marinated chicken in a small baking dish. Bake at 400°F for 20-25 minutes until cooked through. Rest 5 minutes, then shred.
   - **Skillet**: Heat a skillet over medium-high. Cook chicken 5-6 minutes per side. Rest 5 minutes, then shred and return to pan with 2 tbsp water to loosen marinade bits.

3. **Make Cauliflower Rice**:
   - Pulse cauliflower florets in a food processor until broken down into rice-sized pieces.
   - Heat avocado oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add cauliflower rice in a single layer.
   - Cook undisturbed 3 minutes until the bottom starts to brown. Stir and cook another 2-3 minutes until most moisture has evaporated and the rice is lightly golden.
   - Season with cumin, garlic powder, lime juice, and salt. Toss and remove from heat.

4. **Quick Pico**:
   - Toss diced tomatoes and red onion with lime juice and a pinch of salt. Set aside.

5. **Assemble**:
   - Lay butter lettuce cups on a plate.
   - Spoon cauliflower rice into each cup as the base.
   - Top with shredded chipotle chicken.
   - Add a spoonful of pico, jalapeño slices, radishes, and fresh cilantro.
   - Serve with lime wedges for squeezing.

## Notes

- **Calories**: ~390 total (chicken ~250 + cauliflower rice ~75 + lettuce ~15 + tomatoes ~25 + onion and jalapeño ~15 + oil ~10) — varies slightly.
- Half a can of chipotles is genuinely hot — the marinade will be bold. Taste the chicken after cooking and adjust with a squeeze of lime if you need to cut the heat slightly.
- The other half of the chipotle can is used in the [chipotle beef chuck chili](chipotle-beef-chuck-chili.md).
- Leftover chicken reheats in a skillet with a splash of water over medium heat.
