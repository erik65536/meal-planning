# Vegetable Frittata

A single-serving oven-finished frittata packed with bell peppers, mushrooms, spinach, and onion. Bold smoked paprika and Italian herb seasoning keeps this from being bland. High protein, genuinely filling, and very low calorie. Easy to make — the oven does the final work.

## Ingredients

### Eggs

- 4 large eggs
- 2 tbsp low-fat milk (or water for fewer calories)
- Salt & Black Pepper
- 0.5 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.5 tsp garlic powder
- 0.5 tsp Italian seasoning
- 0.25 tsp onion powder

### Vegetables

- 0.5 red or orange bell pepper, diced small
- 0.5 cup cremini or white mushrooms, thinly sliced
- 2 cups baby spinach (packed)
- 0.25 medium yellow onion, diced small
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 0.5 tsp avocado oil
- Salt & Black Pepper
- 0.25 tsp smoked paprika (for vegetables)
- 0.25 tsp dried thyme

### Optional Toppings

- [Optional] 1 oz crumbled feta cheese (adds ~75 cal)
- [Optional] Red pepper flakes
- [Optional] Fresh herbs (parsley, basil, or chives) for garnish

**Prep Notes:**

- Use an 8-inch oven-safe skillet (cast iron or stainless) for a proper single-serving frittata.
- Whisk eggs vigorously until fully combined and slightly frothy — this makes the frittata lighter.
- Don't overcook the vegetables before adding the eggs; they'll continue cooking in the oven.
- The frittata is done when the center is just barely set — it firms up slightly as it cools.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Preheat Oven**: 375°F (190°C).

2. **Whisk Eggs**:
   - In a bowl, crack 4 eggs and add milk, smoked paprika, garlic powder, Italian seasoning, onion powder, salt, and pepper.
   - Whisk vigorously until fully combined, frothy, and uniform in color.
   - Set aside.

3. **Sauté Vegetables**:
   - Heat avocado oil in an 8-inch oven-safe skillet over medium heat.
   - Add diced onion. Cook 3-4 minutes until softened.
   - Add bell pepper and mushrooms. Season with salt, pepper, smoked paprika, and thyme.
   - Cook 4-5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until mushrooms release moisture and peppers soften.
   - Add minced garlic and spinach. Cook 60-90 seconds until spinach wilts. Stir frequently.
   - Taste the vegetables and adjust seasoning — they should be well-seasoned on their own.

4. **Add Eggs**:
   - Spread vegetables evenly across the skillet.
   - Pour egg mixture over the vegetables, then gently shake the pan to distribute evenly.
   - Cook on the stovetop, undisturbed, for 2 minutes until the edges just start to set.

5. **Finish in Oven**:
   - Transfer skillet to the oven.
   - Bake at 375°F for 8-10 minutes until the center is just set and the top has a slight golden color.
   - Check at 8 minutes — the frittata should not jiggle when you gently shake the pan.

6. **Rest & Serve**:
   - Let rest in the pan for 2 minutes before slicing.
   - Slide onto a plate or serve directly from the skillet.
   - Top with feta (if using) and fresh herbs.

## Notes

- **Portion**: Serves 1 for dinner or 2 for a light meal. Eat half now, refrigerate the other half for a convenient next-day lunch.
- **Calories**: ~290 without feta; ~365 with 1 oz feta. Eggs are calorie-dense but highly satiating protein — 4 eggs is legitimately filling.
- **8-Inch Skillet**: Key for a single-serving frittata. A larger pan spreads the eggs too thin.
- **Cast Iron**: Works great here and goes from stovetop to oven seamlessly. If using stainless, make sure the handle is oven-safe.
- **Reheating**: Refrigerate leftovers and reheat in the microwave for 60-90 seconds or in a warm skillet. Frittata reheats much better than scrambled eggs.
