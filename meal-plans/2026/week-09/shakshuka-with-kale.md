# Shakshuka with Kale

Eggs poached in a deeply spiced, tomato-based sauce with wilted kale. Bold North African flavors with cumin, smoked paprika, and cayenne. High protein, high volume, very low calorie — the sauce itself is the meal.

## Ingredients

### Sauce Base

- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 0.5 medium yellow onion, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes (or crushed tomatoes)
- 0.25 cup water or vegetable broth
- 1.25 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.5 tsp coriander
- 0.5 tsp chili powder
- 0.25 tsp cayenne (adjust to taste)
- 0.5 tsp dried oregano
- Salt & Black Pepper

### Greens & Eggs

- 3 cups green cabbage, thinly sliced or shredded
- 4 large eggs

### For Serving

- 2 tbsp Greek yogurt, dolloped on top
- 0.25 tsp smoked paprika (for garnish)
- Fresh parsley or cilantro, chopped (optional)
- Lemon wedge

**Prep Notes:**

- Tomato paste is key — bloom it with the spices for 1-2 minutes before adding tomatoes. This deepens the flavor dramatically.
- Use a wide skillet with a lid — you need surface area to space the eggs.
- [Prep tips about greens can be adjusted as needed for cabbage]

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Build the Sauce Base**:
   - Heat olive oil in a wide skillet over medium heat.
   - Add diced onion and cook 5-6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened and translucent.
   - Add minced garlic and cook 1 minute until fragrant.

2. **Bloom Tomato Paste & Spices**:
   - Add tomato paste to the pan. Stir and cook 2 minutes, allowing it to darken slightly.
   - Add cumin, smoked paprika, coriander, chili powder, cayenne, and oregano. Stir everything together and cook 1 more minute until the spices are fragrant.

3. **Add Tomatoes & Greens**:
   - Pour in diced tomatoes and water (or broth). Stir well to combine.
   - Add sliced cabbage, stirring to incorporate into the sauce.
   - Bring to a gentle simmer and cook 8-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the cabbage is softened and sauce has thickened slightly.
   - Taste and adjust salt, pepper, and cayenne.

4. **Poach the Eggs**:
   - Using a spoon, make 4 small wells in the sauce, spaced evenly.
   - Gently crack one egg into each well.
   - Season the tops of the eggs lightly with salt and pepper.
   - Cover the skillet with a lid and simmer gently for 5-7 minutes until egg whites are fully set but yolks are still soft. For firmer yolks, cook 2-3 minutes longer.

5. **Serve**:
   - Remove from heat immediately.
   - Dollop Greek yogurt in the center and dust with a pinch of smoked paprika.
   - Squeeze a lemon wedge over the top.
   - Garnish with fresh parsley or cilantro if desired.
   - Serve directly from the skillet.

## Notes

- **Portion**: 4 eggs in sauce — single serving dinner.
- **Calories**: ~300 (eggs, olive oil, canned tomatoes — sauce is very low cal).
- **Tomato Paste**: Using tomato paste instead of jarred marinara lets you control the flavor more precisely and adds deeper, richer tomato flavor.
- **Spice Level**: Cayenne is adjustable. Start conservative and taste the sauce before adding eggs.
- **No Bread**: Resist the urge to serve with bread — the sauce and eggs are filling enough and skipping bread keeps this very low calorie.
- **Leftovers**: Store leftover sauce (without eggs) and poach fresh eggs when reheating. Poached eggs don't reheat well.
