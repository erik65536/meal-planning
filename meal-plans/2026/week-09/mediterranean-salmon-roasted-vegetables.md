# Mediterranean Salmon with Roasted Asparagus & Cherry Tomatoes

Quick weeknight meal using pre-seasoned Mediterranean salmon with bold roasted vegetables. Essentially zero prep — the marinade does all the work.

## Ingredients

### Protein

- 1 Mediterranean pre-seasoned salmon filet (frozen), cooked from frozen per package instructions
- Cooking spray or 0.5 tsp avocado oil

### Roasted Vegetables

- 0.5 bunch asparagus (about 12-15 spears), woody ends snapped off
- 1 cup cherry tomatoes
- 3 cloves garlic, smashed
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 0.5 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.5 tsp dried oregano
- 0.25 tsp red pepper flakes
- Salt & Black Pepper
- Juice of 0.5 lemon

### For Serving

- Lemon wedges
- Fresh parsley or dill, chopped (optional)

**Prep Notes:**

- No need to thaw the salmon — cook directly from frozen per package directions.
- Snap asparagus at the natural break point to remove woody ends; don't bother cutting with a knife.
- Cherry tomatoes burst and get jammy in the oven — leave them whole.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Preheat Oven**: 400°F (205°C). Line a sheet pan with foil or parchment.

2. **Prep Vegetables**:
   - Toss asparagus and cherry tomatoes with olive oil, smoked paprika, oregano, red pepper flakes, salt, and pepper.
   - Add smashed garlic cloves.
   - Spread in a single layer on the sheet pan, leaving space for the salmon.

3. **Add Salmon**:
   - Lightly coat the sheet pan (or a small section) with cooking spray.
   - Place salmon filet on the pan alongside the vegetables.
   - No additional seasoning needed — the pre-marinated coating is the flavor.

4. **Roast**:
   - Roast at 400°F for 12-15 minutes until salmon is cooked through (flakes easily with a fork) and asparagus is tender with slightly crispy tips.
   - Cherry tomatoes should be blistered and starting to burst.

5. **Serve**:
   - Squeeze fresh lemon over everything.
   - Garnish with chopped parsley or dill if desired.
   - Plate salmon over or alongside the roasted vegetables.

## Notes

- **Portion**: Single serving — 1 filet + generous vegetables.
- **Calories**: ~350 (varies by salmon brand/marinade — check package).
- **Omega-3**: Salmon is one of the best sources; Mediterranean seasoning adds anti-inflammatory herbs.
- **Leftovers**: If the filet is large, refrigerate half and flake it over a salad the next day.
- **Air Fryer Option**: Cook salmon at 390°F for 10-12 minutes from frozen. Roast vegetables in the oven simultaneously.
