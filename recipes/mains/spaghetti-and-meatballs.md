# Spaghetti and Meatballs

A bold, richly seasoned Italian-American classic made low-carb with shirataki (miracle) noodles and a deeply flavored homemade tomato sauce. Tender meatballs built on a panade and finished in a rustic crushed-tomato sauce — quick on a weeknight, extraordinary after a long slow simmer.

## Ingredients

### Meatballs

- 8 oz lean ground beef (80/20 or leaner)
- [Optional] 4 oz ground pork (swap for half the beef for a richer, more traditional flavor)
- 1 slice bread (white or whole wheat), torn into small pieces
- 3 tbsp milk (whole or 2%)
- 1 egg yolk (or 1 small whole egg)
- 3 cloves garlic, finely grated or minced
- 2 tbsp fresh parsley, finely chopped (or 1 tsp dried)
- 0.5 tsp dried oregano
- 0.5 tsp fennel seeds, crushed
- 0.5 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.5 tsp onion powder
- 0.25 tsp red pepper flakes (increase to taste)
- 0.5 tsp black pepper
- 0.5 tsp salt
- [Optional] 2 tbsp grated Parmesan

### Tomato Sauce

- 1 can (28 oz) whole peeled tomatoes, hand-crushed
- 1-2 medium fresh tomatoes, finely diced
- 0.5 medium onion, finely diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tbsp tomato paste
- 0.5 tsp dried oregano
- 0.5 tsp dried basil (or 6-8 fresh basil leaves, torn, added at end)
- 0.5 tsp fennel seeds, crushed
- 0.25-0.5 tsp red pepper flakes
- 0.5 tsp black pepper
- [Optional] 0.25 cup dry red wine (deglaze after onions)
- [Optional] 1 tsp fish sauce or Worcestershire sauce (umami depth)
- [Optional] 1 tsp balsamic vinegar (stir in at end, brightens flavor)
- Salt to taste

### Noodles

- 1 package (7-8 oz) shirataki noodles, spaghetti style
- [Optional] Zucchini noodles (zoodles) as additional bulk

### Toppings

- Fresh basil, torn
- [Optional] Grated Parmesan or Pecorino Romano
- Red pepper flakes
- Fresh parsley, chopped

**Prep Notes:**

- **Panade**: Combine torn bread and milk in a bowl, mash into a smooth paste with a fork, and let sit 2-3 minutes before mixing into the meat. This is the key to a moist, tender meatball — without it, lean beef meatballs turn dense and dry.
- **Panade alternative**: Substitute 3 tbsp plain or Italian breadcrumbs + 2 tbsp milk for a slightly firmer texture.
- **Mixing meatballs**: Mix with hands until just combined. Overworking activates the proteins and makes tough, rubbery meatballs. Stop as soon as everything is incorporated.
- **Hand-crushing tomatoes**: Squeeze whole canned tomatoes directly into the pot (or into a bowl first) by hand to get a chunky, rustic texture with uneven bits — far better than pre-crushed.
- **Shirataki noodles**: These must be dry-fried. Drain, rinse thoroughly under cold water, then cook in a dry hot skillet (no oil, medium-high) for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly, until squeaky and mostly dry. This removes the natural konjac odor and creates a more pasta-like texture.

**Variations:**

- **Beef-pork mix**: Replace half the ground beef with ground pork for a richer, sweeter meatball — the traditional Italian-American approach.
- **Meat sauce (no meatballs)**: See the Meat Sauce Variation section below for a Bolognese-style adaptation.

## Seasoning Variations

### Classic Italian-American
The bold, familiar profile — fennel, oregano, garlic, and basil up front:
- Meatballs: 0.5 tsp fennel seeds (crushed), 1 tsp dried oregano, 0.5 tsp garlic powder, 0.5 tsp onion powder, 0.25 tsp red pepper flakes, 2 tbsp fresh parsley
- Sauce: 1 tsp dried oregano, 0.5 tsp dried basil, 0.5 tsp fennel seeds, 0.25 tsp red pepper flakes, finish with torn fresh basil

### Spicy Arrabbiata
All heat and garlic — aggressive, no-apologies spice:
- Double the red pepper flakes in both meatballs and sauce (0.5 tsp meatballs, 1 tsp+ sauce)
- Use 6 cloves garlic in the sauce
- Add 0.25 tsp cayenne to meatballs
- Finish sauce with 1 tsp balsamic vinegar and fresh parsley (not basil)
- Omit fennel for a cleaner heat profile

### Herb Garden
Bright, green, and fresh — built around fresh herbs:
- Meatballs: 0.25 cup fresh parsley + 2 tbsp fresh basil, 1 tsp lemon zest, 0.25 tsp dried thyme
- Sauce: 1 tsp dried thyme, 1 tsp dried rosemary (finely crushed), 0.5 tsp dried marjoram, generous finish of fresh basil and parsley
- Finish with a squeeze of fresh lemon juice over the plated dish

### Sunday Gravy
Rich, slow-developed, deeply savory — the all-day version:
- Add 0.5 cup dry red wine to sauce after onions; reduce fully before adding tomatoes
- Add 1 tsp fish sauce or Worcestershire to sauce
- Add a pinch of cinnamon and cloves to meatballs (subtle but traditional)
- Must use the long simmer option — this profile requires 1.5-2+ hours to develop properly
- Finish with a small drizzle of good olive oil

### Sicilian
Briny, complex, sweet-savory with North African influence:
- Sauce: Add 2 tbsp capers, 0.25 cup chopped green olives, and a pinch of cinnamon
- Meatballs: Add a pinch of nutmeg and cinnamon, 2 tbsp raisins (finely chopped, optional), 0.25 tsp allspice
- Add 1 tsp honey to the sauce to balance the brine and acidity
- Garnish with toasted pine nuts

## Cooking Method Variations

### Meatball Cooking Methods

**Pan-Fried (Stovetop)**
Best for: Deep Maillard crust, bold sear flavor, quick:
- Heat 1 tbsp avocado oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat
- Add meatballs with space between them; do not crowd
- Brown on all sides, turning every 60-90 seconds (3-4 minutes total)
- They don't need to be cooked through — they'll finish in the sauce
- Deglaze the pan with a splash of wine or water and pour the fond into the sauce

**Oven-Baked**
Best for: Hands-off cooking, even browning, less active time:
- Preheat oven to 400°F
- Arrange meatballs on a foil-lined sheet pan with a light spray of oil
- Bake 18-22 minutes until cooked through (internal temp 165°F)
- Optional: broil for the final 2 minutes for extra color

**Broiled**
Best for: Fast crust with minimal oil, no standing at the stove:
- Place meatballs on a foil-lined pan, lightly oiled
- Broil on HIGH, 4-5 inches from the broiler element
- 8-10 minutes total, turning once at the halfway mark
- Watch closely — they go from golden to burnt fast

**Simmered Directly in Sauce**
Best for: Maximum tenderness, deepest flavor integration, no browning:
- Once the sauce has been simmering 15+ minutes, gently nestle raw meatballs into the sauce
- Partially cover and simmer on low for 25-35 minutes, turning meatballs once gently
- No crust forms, but the texture becomes meltingly soft and the meatballs take on all the sauce flavor
- Best paired with the long simmer option

### Sauce Simmer Options

**Quick Sauce (20-30 minutes)**
- After adding tomatoes, simmer uncovered on medium-low for 20-30 minutes
- Brighter, more acidic tomato flavor with lighter body
- Best for weeknight cooking; still very good

**Long Sauce (1.5-2+ hours)**
- Simmer covered on very low heat for 45 minutes, then uncover for the final 30-60 minutes
- Sauce becomes deeply sweet, jammy, and complex — acidity mellows completely
- Meatballs simmered directly in the sauce become almost fork-tender
- Stir every 20-30 minutes; reduce heat further if spattering
- This is the Sunday version — start it early and let it go

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Make the panade**: Tear bread into small pieces into a bowl. Pour milk over and mash into a paste with a fork. Let sit 2-3 minutes.
2. **Mix meatballs**: Add ground beef (and pork if using), egg yolk, garlic, parsley, and all meatball seasonings to the panade. Mix gently with hands until just combined — stop immediately when incorporated.
3. **Form meatballs**: Roll into 1.5-inch balls (about the size of a large walnut). Wet hands prevent sticking. Yields 12-16 meatballs. Refrigerate while building the sauce if not cooking immediately.
4. **Cook meatballs**: Use one of the methods from Cooking Method Variations above. Set browned or baked meatballs aside.
5. **Build the sauce**:
    - Heat olive oil in a large deep skillet or Dutch oven over medium heat.
    - Add onion. Cook 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until soft and golden at the edges.
    - Add garlic and tomato paste. Cook 1-2 minutes, stirring, until the paste darkens slightly and smells nutty.
    - If using red wine, add now. Let it bubble and reduce for 2 minutes, scraping up any browned bits.
    - Add hand-crushed whole tomatoes with their juices and the fresh diced tomatoes. Stir to combine.
    - Add oregano, basil, fennel seeds, red pepper flakes, black pepper, and fish sauce/Worcestershire if using.
6. **Simmer sauce**: Choose your simmer time from the Sauce Simmer Options above.
7. **Finish with meatballs**: If the meatballs were browned or baked (not simmered directly in sauce from the start), nestle them into the sauce for the final 15-20 minutes of simmering to absorb flavor and finish cooking.
8. **Prepare shirataki noodles**: While the sauce simmers, drain and rinse noodles under cold water for 30 seconds. Dry-fry in a hot skillet over medium-high heat (no oil) for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly, until squeaky and dry. Season lightly with salt.
9. **Finish sauce**: Taste and adjust salt. Stir in balsamic vinegar if using. Add torn fresh basil.
10. **Serve**: Plate noodles, spoon sauce and meatballs over top generously. Finish with Parmesan, fresh basil, parsley, and red pepper flakes.

## Meat Sauce Variation (No Meatballs)

A Bolognese-style meat sauce — rich, slow-cooked, and deeply savory. Skip all meatball steps.

**Additional ingredients:**
- 1 small carrot, finely diced
- 1 celery stalk, finely diced
- [Optional] 0.25 cup dry white or red wine
- [Optional] 3 tbsp whole milk or light cream (stirred in at the very end)

**Instructions:**
1. Heat olive oil in a Dutch oven over medium-high. Add ground beef (and pork if using) and cook, breaking up into small crumbles. Do not drain — let fat render into the base for flavor. Cook until fully browned with some crispy bits.
2. Reduce heat to medium. Add onion, carrot, and celery (the soffritto). Cook 8-10 minutes, stirring often, until very soft and beginning to caramelize.
3. Add garlic and tomato paste. Cook 2 minutes.
4. Add wine and reduce 2 minutes.
5. Add crushed tomatoes, fresh tomatoes, and all seasonings. Stir well.
6. Simmer on very low heat for at minimum 45 minutes — ideal is 1.5-2 hours. A true meat sauce gets exponentially better with time. Stir every 20-30 minutes.
7. In the final 5 minutes, stir in milk. This traditional Bolognese finish softens the acidity and adds a subtle richness without heavy cream.
8. Serve over prepared shirataki noodles with Parmesan and fresh parsley.

## Notes

- **Shirataki noodles**: Zero net carbs, very low calorie, made from konjac root. The dry-frying step is non-negotiable — skip it and the noodles stay rubbery and wet with a strong odor.
- **Lycopene**: Long-cooked tomatoes release significantly more lycopene (a powerful antioxidant) than raw or briefly cooked — the long simmer has a genuine nutritional payoff.
- **Sodium**: Canned tomatoes vary widely in sodium content. Use no-salt-added if available and season to taste at the end.
- **Meatball size consistency**: Using a small cookie scoop or tablespoon measure ensures even sizing, which means even cooking time across all meatballs.
