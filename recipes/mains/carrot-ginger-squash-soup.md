# Carrot Ginger Butternut Squash Soup

A silky, deeply flavored pureed soup built on roasted carrots, butternut squash, and fresh ginger. Rich in beta-carotene, anti-inflammatory compounds, and flavor — but remarkably low in calories. Serve alongside a lean protein for a complete, filling dinner.

## Ingredients

### Soup

- 1 cup butternut squash, peeled and cut into 1-inch cubes
- 1.5 cups carrots, peeled and roughly chopped (about 2 large carrots)
- 0.5 medium yellow onion, roughly chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, smashed
- 1 inch fresh ginger, peeled and roughly chopped
- 0.5 tbsp avocado oil
- 1.5 cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
- 0.25 cup light coconut milk (or unsweetened almond milk for lower fat)
- 1 tsp cumin
- 0.5 tsp turmeric
- 0.5 tsp smoked paprika
- 0.25 tsp cayenne (adjust to taste)
- 0.25 tsp coriander
- Salt & Black Pepper

### For Serving

- Fresh cilantro or flat-leaf parsley, chopped
- 1 tbsp plain Greek yogurt or swirl of light coconut milk (optional)
- Red pepper flakes or chili oil drizzle (optional)
- Lime or lemon wedge

**Prep Notes:**

- Roasting the vegetables before blending adds substantial depth — don't skip this step if you have time.
- Fresh ginger is strongly preferred over ground for this recipe; the brightness doesn't come through the same way with dried.
- An immersion blender works well and saves dishes; a countertop blender gives a silkier result.
- This recipe scales easily to 2 servings (double all quantities) for the meal-prep approach — make a full pot and reheat the second serving.

**Variations:**

- **Protein Subs**: Baked lemon-herb chicken breast, garlic sautéed shrimp, pan-seared tofu, or white beans stirred directly into the soup.
- **Creamier**: Add 0.5 cup light coconut milk instead of 0.25 cup.
- **Apple Version**: Add 0.5 granny smith apple to roasting pan — adds subtle sweetness and tartness.
- **Spicier**: Add 0.5 tsp harissa paste or extra cayenne.

## Seasoning Variations

- **Classic Warm Spiced**: Cumin, turmeric, coriander, smoked paprika, cayenne (base recipe)
- **Thai-Inspired**: Lemongrass paste (1 tsp), fish sauce (0.5 tsp), lime zest, red curry paste (0.5 tsp); finish with lime juice and cilantro
- **Indian-Spiced**: Garam masala (1 tsp), turmeric, cumin, mustard seeds bloomed in oil first; finish with a squeeze of lemon
- **Simple**: Just salt, pepper, and a generous amount of ginger and garlic — lets the vegetables shine

## Cooking Method Variations

**Roasted Method (Preferred):**

1. Toss squash, carrots, onion, and garlic with oil, salt, and pepper. Roast at 425°F for 25-30 minutes until caramelized.
2. Transfer to a pot with broth, ginger, and spices. Simmer 5 minutes, then blend.

**Stovetop (Faster):**

1. Sauté onion in oil 5 minutes. Add garlic and ginger, cook 1 minute.
2. Add squash, carrots, spices, and broth. Simmer covered 20-25 minutes until vegetables are very tender.
3. Blend until smooth, stir in coconut milk.

**Instant Pot:**

1. Sauté onion in oil on sauté mode. Add garlic, ginger, squash, carrots, spices, and broth.
2. Pressure cook on high for 8 minutes. Quick release. Blend until smooth, stir in coconut milk.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Roast Vegetables**: Preheat oven to 425°F. Toss squash, carrots, onion, and garlic with oil, salt, and pepper. Spread in a single layer on a baking sheet. Roast 25-30 minutes, flipping once, until caramelized and tender.

2. **Build the Soup**: Transfer roasted vegetables to a medium saucepan. Add broth, fresh ginger, and all spices. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook 5 minutes.

3. **Blend**: Remove from heat. Use an immersion blender to blend until completely smooth. Alternatively, transfer in batches to a countertop blender (be careful with hot liquid — vent the lid).

4. **Finish**: Stir in light coconut milk. Return to low heat and simmer 2-3 minutes. Taste and adjust salt, pepper, and cayenne. Add a squeeze of lime or lemon to brighten the flavors.

5. **Serve**: Ladle into a bowl. Garnish with a swirl of Greek yogurt or coconut milk, fresh cilantro or parsley, and a pinch of red pepper flakes. Serve with your chosen protein alongside.

## Notes

- **Calories (Soup Only)**: ~240 cal per serving (with light coconut milk). Without coconut milk ~195 cal.
- **Complete Meal**: Add 4 oz chicken breast (~130 cal), 4 oz shrimp (~110 cal), or pan-seared tofu (~100 cal) to reach 350-370 cal total.
- **Bulk Factor**: Despite being low calorie, the fiber in squash and carrots plus the volume of soup is very filling. Serve in a large bowl.
- **Make Ahead**: Soup stores well in the fridge for 4-5 days. Make a double batch on day 1, reheat for day 2 dinner with a fresh protein.
- **Color**: Turmeric gives this a vivid golden-orange color. Add a pinch more if you want a deeper color.
