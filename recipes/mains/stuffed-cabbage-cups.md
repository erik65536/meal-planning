# Stuffed Cabbage Cups

Green cabbage quarters are separated into natural leaf cups — inspired by stuffed squash — then filled with a savory stuffing of protein, grain, and aromatics, topped, and baked or air fried until tender. Highly adaptable across flavor profiles, proteins, and grains.

## Ingredients

### Cabbage

- 0.25–0.5 head green cabbage (yields 2–4 cups per quarter)

**Prep Notes:**
Quarter the cabbage through the core. Gently press or pry apart the inner layers of each quarter to create 2–3 cup-shaped cavities, leaving some layers attached at the base for structure. For a softer result, blanch cups in boiling salted water 2–3 minutes before stuffing and pat dry. For deeper caramelization, roast empty cups at 425°F for 10–12 minutes before stuffing.

### Protein (choose one)

- 0.25 lb ground beef, turkey, chicken, or pork
- 0.25 lb Italian sausage, casing removed
- 0.25 lb ground lamb
- 0.5 cup shredded rotisserie chicken
- 0.5 cup chickpeas or white beans, rinsed (vegetarian)
- 0.33 cup dry green or brown lentils, cooked (vegetarian)
- 0.5 cup firm tofu, crumbled and pressed (vegetarian)

### Grain (choose one)

- 0.25 cup dry white or brown rice
- 0.25 cup dry quinoa
- 0.25 cup dry couscous
- 0.25 cup dry bulgur wheat
- 0.25 cup dry farro
- 0.25 cup dry orzo
- 0.5 cup cauliflower rice (low-carb)

**Prep Notes:**
Cook grain according to package directions before mixing into filling. Par-cooking in seasoned broth instead of water adds depth. Couscous can be hydrated directly in the hot filling liquid.

### Aromatics

- 2–3 cloves garlic, minced
- 0.5 medium onion, finely diced
- 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated, or 0.5 tsp ground (for Asian or curry profiles)
- 1–2 stalks celery, diced [Optional, for savory Western profiles]

### Add-ins (choose 1–3)

**Vegetables:**
- 0.5 cup diced tomatoes, canned or fresh
- 0.5 cup mushrooms, finely diced
- 0.25 cup frozen peas or corn
- 0.25 cup roasted red peppers, diced
- 0.25 cup zucchini, diced
- Handful of spinach or kale, roughly chopped
- 0.25 cup sun-dried tomatoes, chopped
- 0.25 cup artichoke hearts, chopped

**Beans & Legumes:**
- 0.25 cup black beans, kidney beans, or chickpeas, rinsed

**Dried Fruit (sweet-savory profiles):**
- 2–3 tbsp dried cranberries, raisins, golden raisins, diced dried apricots, or currants

**Nuts:**
- 2–3 tbsp toasted pine nuts, slivered almonds, chopped walnuts, or chopped pecans

**Briny:**
- 1–2 tbsp capers
- 2–3 tbsp sliced kalamata olives

### Sauce / Liquid Binder (choose one)

- 0.25 cup tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
- 0.25 cup light coconut milk
- 0.25 cup chicken or vegetable broth
- 2 tbsp soy sauce + 1 tsp sesame oil + 3 tbsp broth
- 2 tbsp peanut or almond butter whisked with 3 tbsp warm broth
- 2 tbsp harissa paste + 3 tbsp broth

**Prep Notes:**
The liquid binder keeps the filling moist during baking and helps it hold together. The filling should be moist but not soupy before stuffing — if too wet, cook off excess liquid in the skillet before filling the cups.

### Topping (choose 1–2, applied before cooking)

- Panko breadcrumbs (2–3 tbsp)
- Regular breadcrumbs (2–3 tbsp)
- Crushed walnuts, almonds, or pecans (2 tbsp)
- Grated parmesan or gruyere (1–2 tbsp)
- Crumbled feta (1–2 tbsp)
- Wheat germ (2 tbsp)

**Variations:**
- Panko + parmesan (classic, Italian profiles)
- Panko + crushed almonds (curry, Moroccan profiles)
- Wheat germ + crushed pecans (earthy, nut butter profiles)
- Feta + panko (Greek profiles)

**Prep Notes:**
Lightly drizzle or spray topping with olive oil before baking to encourage browning and crispness.

### Garnish (choose 1–2, added after cooking)

- Plain Greek yogurt or plain yogurt (2–3 tbsp)
- Fresh cilantro, parsley, dill, mint, or basil
- Sliced green onions
- Sesame seeds, pumpkin seeds, or sunflower seeds
- Lemon or lime wedge or zest
- Hot sauce, sriracha, or chili crisp
- Pomegranate molasses drizzle
- Tahini drizzle
- Fresh diced tomato or pico de gallo

## Seasoning Variations

**Curry / Indian:**
1 tsp curry powder, 0.5 tsp turmeric, 0.5 tsp cumin, 0.5 tsp coriander, 0.25 tsp garam masala, 0.25 tsp ground ginger, salt to taste. Use light coconut milk as binder. Add chickpeas and golden raisins. Top with wheat germ + crushed almonds. Garnish with Greek yogurt, fresh cilantro, and a squeeze of lemon.

**Mexican:**
1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp chili powder, 0.5 tsp smoked paprika, 0.5 tsp dried oregano, 0.25 tsp cayenne, 1 tsp garlic powder, salt to taste. Use tomato sauce + lime juice as binder. Add black beans and corn. Top with panko + shredded pepper jack. Garnish with Greek yogurt (as sour cream substitute), pico de gallo, and green onions.

**Italian / Marinara:**
1 tsp dried oregano, 0.5 tsp dried basil, 0.25 tsp fennel seed, 0.25 tsp red pepper flakes, 3 cloves garlic, salt to taste. Use tomato sauce as binder. Add olives, sun-dried tomatoes, or capers. Top with panko + parmesan. Garnish with fresh basil and lemon zest.

**Korean:**
1 tbsp gochujang, 1 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tsp sesame oil, 1 tsp rice vinegar, 0.5 tsp grated ginger, 2 cloves garlic. Use soy + sesame broth as binder. Add mushrooms and diced zucchini. Top with panko + sesame seeds. Garnish with sliced green onions, sesame seeds, and a drizzle of chili crisp.

**Moroccan / Middle Eastern:**
1 tsp ras el hanout or baharat, 0.5 tsp ground cinnamon, 0.5 tsp cumin, 0.25 tsp allspice, pinch of cayenne, salt to taste. Use tomato-broth binder. Add dried apricots or raisins, pine nuts, and chickpeas. Top with crushed almonds + wheat germ. Garnish with Greek yogurt, pomegranate molasses drizzle, and fresh parsley.

**Nut Butter / Asian-Inspired:**
2 tbsp peanut or almond butter whisked with 3 tbsp broth, 1 tbsp soy sauce, 0.5 tsp sesame oil, 1 tsp rice vinegar, 0.5 tsp grated ginger, 0.5 tsp garlic, dash of sriracha. Add edamame or frozen peas, shredded carrots. Top with panko + crushed peanuts or almonds. Garnish with green onions, fresh cilantro, lime wedge, and sesame seeds.

**Greek / Lemon Herb:**
1 tsp dried oregano, 0.5 tsp dried dill, zest and juice of 0.5 lemon, 3 cloves garlic, salt to taste. Use broth + lemon juice as binder. Add kalamata olives, roasted red peppers, and spinach. Top with feta + panko. Garnish with fresh dill, lemon zest, and Greek yogurt.

**Smoky BBQ:**
1 tsp smoked paprika, 0.5 tsp chipotle powder or chili powder, 0.5 tsp cumin, 0.5 tsp garlic powder, 0.25 tsp onion powder, 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce, salt to taste. Use a splash of broth + small amount of tomato sauce as binder. Add corn, black beans, or diced roasted red pepper. Top with panko + sharp cheddar. Garnish with green onions and a drizzle of hot sauce.

## Cooking Method Variations

### Oven Baked (Standard)
Preheat oven to 375°F. Arrange stuffed cups in a baking dish or rimmed sheet pan. Add a small splash of broth or water to the pan for steam. Cover tightly with foil and bake 20–25 minutes until cabbage is tender. Remove foil, add topping, and bake uncovered 10–15 minutes more until topping is golden.

### Air Fryer
Preheat air fryer to 370°F. Arrange stuffed cups in a single layer (work in batches if needed). Air fry 12–15 minutes until cabbage edges are tender and topping is browned. If topping browns too quickly, tent loosely with foil for the first half.

### Stovetop + Broil Finish
Bake uncovered in an oven-safe skillet at 400°F for 20 minutes, then switch to broil on high for 2–4 minutes to crisp the topping. Watch closely to avoid burning.

### Pre-roasted Cups
For deeper caramelization on the cabbage: brush empty cups lightly with avocado oil, season with salt and pepper, and roast at 425°F for 10–12 minutes. Remove, stuff, add topping, and continue baking at 375°F for 15–20 minutes uncovered.

### Blanched Cups (Softer Result)
For a silkier, more tender cabbage texture: blanch quartered cups in boiling salted water 2–3 minutes. Drain and pat dry before stuffing. Bake as in the standard method but reduce covered bake time to 15 minutes since the cabbage is already par-cooked.

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Prepare the cabbage.** Quarter the head through the core. Gently separate the inner layers of each quarter into cup shapes, keeping some layers attached at the base. Optionally blanch or pre-roast (see variations above). Arrange on a baking dish or sheet pan.

2. **Cook the aromatics.** Heat 1 tsp olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Sauté onion 3–4 minutes until softened. Add garlic (and ginger if using) and cook 1 minute more.

3. **Brown the protein.** Add protein and cook, breaking it apart, until browned and cooked through. Drain excess fat if needed. (Skip this step if using pre-cooked chicken or canned legumes — add them with the grain instead.)

4. **Build the filling.** Stir in spices and cook 30 seconds until fragrant. Add the liquid binder, pre-cooked grain, and any add-ins (vegetables, dried fruit, nuts, beans). Stir to combine and cook 2–3 minutes. Taste and season generously — filling should be bold and well-seasoned. Add a squeeze of acid (lemon juice or vinegar) to brighten if needed.

5. **Stuff the cups.** Spoon filling into each cup, pressing gently to pack. Mound slightly above the rim.

6. **Add topping.** Sprinkle topping evenly over each cup. Lightly drizzle or spray with olive oil.

7. **Cook.** Follow the chosen cooking method variation above.

8. **Garnish and serve.** Let rest 2–3 minutes. Add garnishes (yogurt, fresh herbs, seeds, sauces) just before serving.

## Notes

- A single quarter of a medium cabbage head typically yields 2–3 cups and is a satisfying single serving when stuffed.
- Cabbage cups can be prepped (quartered and separated) a day ahead and refrigerated, covered.
- The filling can be made up to 2 days ahead; stuff just before baking.
- Leftover filling works well as a grain bowl with extra vegetables.
- For lower calorie, higher volume: increase the ratio of vegetables and use cauliflower rice; reduce or omit nuts and dried fruit.
- If buying a full head of cabbage, plan other meals that week to use the remaining quarters — raw in slaws, quick-braised as a side, or in soups and stir-fries.
