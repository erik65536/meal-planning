# Cottage Cheese & Oat Protein Pancakes

A three-ingredient, whole-food pancake that is extremely satiating, high in slow-digesting protein, and easy to log accurately. Blending the batter eliminates the curd texture of cottage cheese entirely, producing a smooth batter that cooks like traditional pancakes.

## Ingredients

### Base (makes 3-4 small pancakes, 1 serving)

- 0.5 cup plain low-fat or fat-free cottage cheese
- 0.25 cup rolled oats
- 2 large egg whites (or 3 tbsp liquid egg whites)
- 0.5 tsp cinnamon
- 0.5 tsp vanilla extract [Optional]
- [Optional] tiny pinch of baking powder for extra fluff

**Prep Notes:**

- **Blending is key**: Blend on high for 15-20 seconds until completely smooth. Do not stir by hand — you need to break down the oats and cottage cheese curds fully.
- Keep pancakes small (3-4 inches wide) so they are easy to flip without breaking.
- A non-stick pan is strongly preferred; cast iron works but requires a bit more oil.

**Variations:**

- **Gluten-free**: Use certified gluten-free rolled oats.
- **Whole egg**: Substitute 1 whole egg for the 2 egg whites to add richness and calories.
- **Higher protein**: Blend in 1 scoop unflavored or vanilla whey/casein protein powder (add 1-2 tbsp water if the batter thickens too much).
- **Banana**: Add 0.5 a ripe banana to the blender for natural sweetness and potassium; reduces cinnamon if desired.
- **Pumpkin spice**: Replace cinnamon with 0.5 tsp pumpkin pie spice; add 2 tbsp canned pumpkin puree.
- **Chocolate**: Add 1 tsp unsweetened cocoa powder and a pinch of espresso powder; pair with sliced banana on top.
- **Lemon ricotta-style**: Substitute cottage cheese with part-skim ricotta; add 0.5 tsp lemon zest and a squeeze of lemon juice.

## Seasoning Variations

- **Classic Cinnamon**: 0.5 tsp cinnamon + 0.5 tsp vanilla extract
- **Warm Spice**: 0.25 tsp cinnamon + 0.25 tsp cardamom + pinch of nutmeg + 0.5 tsp vanilla
- **Tropical**: 0.25 tsp cinnamon + 0.25 tsp ginger + 0.5 tsp coconut extract (use light coconut milk instead of plain cottage cheese for a stronger profile)
- **Savory**: Omit vanilla and cinnamon; add 0.25 tsp garlic powder, pinch of black pepper, and 1 tbsp fresh chives — top with a fried egg or smoked salmon

## Cooking Instructions

1. **Blend the batter**: Add cottage cheese, rolled oats, egg whites, cinnamon, and vanilla to a blender or food processor. Blend on high for 15-20 seconds until the mixture is completely smooth and resembles traditional pancake batter.
2. **Heat the pan**: Place a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Lightly coat with cooking spray or a minimal wipe of avocado oil.
3. **Pour and cook**: Pour batter into the skillet to form small pancakes about 3-4 inches wide. Do not crowd the pan.
4. **Flip**: Cook until edges look set and small bubbles appear on the surface, about 2-3 minutes. Flip gently with a thin spatula and cook another 1-2 minutes until golden brown.
5. **Serve immediately**: These pancakes are best fresh off the pan.

## Toppings

- **Smashed berry jam**: Microwave a small handful of frozen blueberries or raspberries for 30-40 seconds, then mash with a fork. Pour the warm fruit syrup over the top.
- **Cinnamon "frosting"**: Mix 1 tbsp plain Greek yogurt with a heavy dash of cinnamon and a tiny splash of vanilla; spread over warm pancakes.
- **Fresh fruit**: Sliced strawberries, banana, or peaches.
- **Nut butter drizzle**: A thin drizzle (0.5 tsp) of almond or peanut butter for added healthy fat; adds calories so use sparingly if keeping low-calorie.
- **Unsweetened applesauce**: A spoonful on top adds moisture and mild sweetness with minimal calories.

## Notes

- **Macros (approximate, base recipe)**: ~200 calories, ~25g protein, ~18g carbs, ~3g fat — exact values depend on cottage cheese brand.
- **MyFitnessPal tip**: Because this is only 3-4 whole-food ingredients, logging is very fast and accurate. Log cottage cheese, rolled oats, and egg whites as individual entries.
- **Batch prep**: Double or triple the recipe and store cooked pancakes in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat in a dry skillet or toaster for 1-2 minutes.
- **Why small pancakes?**: The batter is denser than traditional batter and more prone to sticking mid-flip. Keeping them small (3-4 inches) dramatically improves the success rate.
